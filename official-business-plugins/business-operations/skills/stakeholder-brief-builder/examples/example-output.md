# Pricing Restructure — Effective 01/08/2026

> **TL;DR**
> - We are increasing subscription pricing by an average of 22% across all tiers, effective 01/08/2026.
> - Existing customers receive a 90-day grace period at current pricing; new customers move to new pricing from 01/06/2026.
> - The increase is required to restore gross margin to 78% and fund the compliance-automation roadmap approved at the February board meeting.

---

## Headline

Effective 01/08/2026, Groundwork HR will move to a restructured three-tier pricing model. Entry pricing increases from $99 to $119/month; Professional from $249 to $299/month; Enterprise moves to negotiated annual contracts with a floor of $18,000/year. The change is required to restore gross margin degraded by 7 percentage points since 2024 and to fund the $340,000 compliance-automation investment approved in February.

---

## Context

**Situation:** Groundwork HR's current pricing has been unchanged since October 2023. At launch, gross margin was 74%. The business now processes 3× the data volume per account, support costs have increased 28%, and AWS infrastructure costs have risen 19% since 2023.

**What changed:** As of Q1 2026, gross margin has fallen to 67% — 11 points below the SaaS benchmark of 78% and below the level required to fund the compliance-automation roadmap. A pricing analysis (March 2026) confirmed Groundwork HR is priced 18–24% below direct competitors Employsure and HR Partner at equivalent feature tiers.

---

## What We Are Doing

We are implementing a three-tier pricing structure effective 01/08/2026 for all new customers from 01/06/2026. Existing customers will be notified by 01/06/2026 and given a 90-day grace period at current pricing, transitioning on 01/09/2026. Enterprise customers will be migrated to annual contracts individually through account management from 01/06/2026.

The new structure is:

| Tier | Current | New | Change |
|------|---------|-----|--------|
| Starter (up to 50 employees) | $99/month | $119/month | +$20 (+20%) |
| Professional (51–150 employees) | $249/month | $299/month | +$50 (+20%) |
| Enterprise (151+ employees) | $499/month | Annual contract ≥ $18,000/year | +~21% average |

A Van Westendorp price sensitivity analysis conducted with 48 customers in April 2026 confirmed the new price points sit within the "acceptable" range for 84% of respondents.

---

## Decision / Ask

The board is asked to **approve the pricing structure and communication timeline** as presented. CEO and Head of Commercial will execute upon approval. No board action is required post-approval unless churn exceeds 5% in the 30 days following announcement.

---

## Risks

- **Customer churn above forecast** — Van Westendorp modelling projects ≤ 3% churn; if churn exceeds 5% within 60 days, management will activate a retention offer (12-month lock-in at 10% discount) without further board approval. Trigger point: 01/08/2026 +30 days.
- **Competitor response** — Employsure and HR Partner may use this as an acquisition opportunity. Mitigant: 90-day grace period and proactive account manager outreach reduces switching incentive window.
- **Communication misstep** — A poorly worded customer email could amplify churn. Mitigant: Draft communications reviewed by Head of CX and external copywriter before release; legal sign-off on terms-of-service update by 23/05/2026.
