# Pricing Update — What It Means for the Team

## Headline

From 01/08/2026, we're updating our subscription pricing for the first time since 2023. Most customers will see a $20–$50/month change. Existing customers get a 90-day grace period. There are **no team restructures or role changes** linked to this update.

---

## Why We're Doing This

Our pricing hasn't moved in nearly three years. In that time:

- Each customer account uses three times the data we originally sized for.
- The cost of running our infrastructure has gone up by close to 20%.
- Our gross margin has slipped from 74% to 67%, which is below where it needs to be to keep funding the compliance-automation work we committed to in February.

Without the price update, we can't keep investing in the platform at the pace we have been.

---

## What's Changing

| Customer tier | Current | New |
|---------------|---------|-----|
| Starter | $99/month | $119/month |
| Professional | $249/month | $299/month |
| Enterprise | $499/month | Annual contracts from $18,000/year |

New customers move to the new pricing from 01/06/2026. Existing customers have a 90-day grace period and shift on 01/09/2026.

---

## What It Means for You

- **Support team** — expect more inbound questions from customers between 01/06 and 30/06. We've drafted FAQ responses for you; Head of CX will run a training session on 28/05.
- **Sales team** — new pricing goes live in Stripe on 01/06. Updated battlecards land 27/05. Quotas for the rest of the year are unchanged.
- **Engineering and product** — no changes to roadmap; the compliance-automation work this funds is already in your sprint plan.
- **Everyone else** — no day-to-day change. If a customer or a friend asks, the message is: "We're investing more in the platform and we've kept pricing flat for three years longer than most SaaS would."

---

## Risks We're Watching

- Some customers may push back. We have a retention offer ready (12-month lock-in at a 10% discount) for high-value accounts. Sales and CX have the authority to use it without escalation.
- A small number of customers may leave. Our modelling says fewer than 3 in every 100; we'll know within 60 days of launch.
