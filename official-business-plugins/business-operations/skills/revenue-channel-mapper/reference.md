## RICE Prioritisation

RICE Score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort

### Scoring Guide

**Reach** (customers per month at scale):
- 1–3: Niche / small audience (< 500/mo)
- 4–6: Mid-size audience (500–5,000/mo)
- 7–10: Large audience (5,000+/mo)

**Impact** (per-customer revenue or conversion impact):
- 1: Marginal — < 10% improvement
- 2: Significant — 10–50% improvement
- 3: Transformational — > 50% improvement

**Confidence** (in the estimates):
- 10%: Gut feel only
- 50%: Some comparable data or prior experiments
- 80%: Strong comparable data
- 100%: Measured directly

**Effort** (person-weeks to run a meaningful 90-day test):
- 1: < 1 week
- 2: 1–2 weeks
- 3: 3–4 weeks
- 4: 5–8 weeks
- 5: > 8 weeks

### Interpretation

| RICE Score | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| > 50 | High priority — invest now |
| 20–50 | Medium priority — schedule in next quarter |
| 5–20 | Low priority — revisit if top channels plateau |
| < 5 | Deprioritise — not worth the effort at this stage |

## Contribution Margin per Channel

**Contribution margin** = Revenue from channel − variable costs attributable to channel (ad spend, sales commission, affiliate fees, fulfilment).

Do NOT subtract fixed costs (product development, shared infrastructure). Fixed costs are period costs, not channel costs.

### Channel Economics Benchmarks (Australian SMB context, 2024)

| Business Type | Typical Blended CAC | Healthy LTV:CAC |
|--------------|--------------------|-----------------:|
| SaaS (B2B, < $10K ACV) | $300–$1,200 | ≥ 3:1 |
| SaaS (B2B, $10K–$50K ACV) | $2,000–$8,000 | ≥ 3:1 |
| eCommerce (B2C, fashion/homewares) | $30–$120 | ≥ 2:1 |
| Professional services (B2B) | $500–$3,000 | ≥ 4:1 |
| Local services (B2C) | $20–$200 | ≥ 2:1 |
| Marketplace (two-sided) | $50–$400 per side | ≥ 2.5:1 |

### LTV:CAC Warning Thresholds

| LTV:CAC | Signal |
|---------|--------|
| < 1.0 | Destroying value — cut immediately unless strategic |
| 1.0–1.5 | Marginal — optimise or deprioritise |
| 1.5–3.0 | Acceptable — room for improvement |
| 3.0–5.0 | Healthy — scale with confidence |
| > 5.0 | Exceptional — investigate if repeatable at scale |

## Friction Score Rubric

Score each channel 0–5 on how hard it is to scale:

| Score | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 0 | Fully automated, no capacity constraints | Email to existing list, viral loop with product |
| 1 | Minimal manual effort, easy to scale budget | SEM, social ads |
| 2 | Some operational overhead, scalable with process | SEO content, affiliate management |
| 3 | Moderate constraint — people or time-limited | Outbound sales, BD partnerships |
| 4 | High constraint — scarce resource or long cycle | Trade shows (fixed dates), speaking engagements |
| 5 | Severely constrained — near-impossible to scale fast | Founder-led sales, bespoke enterprise deals |

## Channel Groupings

For Mermaid diagram and table grouping:

- **Direct**: Sales (outbound), BD, trade shows, speaking
- **Paid**: SEM, social/display ads, offline ads
- **Organic**: SEO, content marketing, community building
- **Earned**: PR, unconventional PR, viral, targeting blogs
- **Partnership**: Affiliate, existing platforms, BD (channel-distribution)
- **Owned**: Email marketing, engineering-as-marketing tools

## 90-Day Experiment Template

```
Hypothesis: If we [specific action], then [metric] will [increase/decrease] by [X%]
             within [timeframe], as evidenced by [measurement method].

Success metric: [Single KPI with target value]
Budget: AUD [amount] + [person-days] of internal effort
Owner: [Role — e.g. Head of Marketing, Growth Manager]
Start: Week [N] of 90-day window
Kill criterion: If [metric] has not moved [Y%] by day [D], stop and document learnings.
```
