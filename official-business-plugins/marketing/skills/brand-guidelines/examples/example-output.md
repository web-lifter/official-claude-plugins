## 1. About These Guidelines

### Purpose
Crema is a Melbourne-based boutique coffee chain with 7 cafés and a wholesale roastery. This brand book is the source of truth for how Crema looks, sounds, and behaves across every customer touchpoint — from cup sleeves to email newsletters to wholesale invoices.

### How to use this document
- **Designers:** Sections 3–6 (Logo, Colour, Typography, Imagery)
- **Copywriters:** Section 7 (Voice and Messaging)
- **Marketers:** Sections 7 and 8 (Voice + Application Examples)
- **Wholesale partners:** Sections 3 and 9 (Logo + Asset Library)
- **Photographers:** Section 6.1 (Photography Style)

### Update cadence
Reviewed quarterly. Last reviewed: 11/04/2026.

### Exception requests
Email maya@crema.coffee with the proposed use case, the rule it bends, and the rationale. Brand owner responds within 5 business days.

## 2. Brand Story

### Mission
> We make every coffee in our shops the way we'd make one for a friend at our own kitchen table — slowly, accurately, and with someone we know in mind.

### Vision
> A Melbourne where every neighbourhood has a café where the staff know your order and the coffee is consistent enough to be boring.

### Core Values

- **Consistency over flair** — A perfect flat white every time matters more than latte art that wins competitions.
- **Train like a craft, not like a job** — Every barista does a 6-week apprenticeship before pulling shots for paying customers.
- **One bag, one story** — Every wholesale bean has a single producer, with the producer's name on the bag. No blends marketed as origin coffee.
- **Slower is honest** — We refuse to install a third-wave robotic bar. Coffee made by humans takes the time it takes.

### Brand Archetype
**Primary:** The Caregiver (with a Sage secondary)
**What this means in practice:** Crema treats its customers and staff like people the founders genuinely care about. The Sage side comes through in the technical precision of the craft and the willingness to explain why things are made a certain way. The brand is warm but never cute.

### Who we serve
- **Daily regulars** — locals within 800m of each shop, 25–55, white-collar or working from home, value consistency over novelty
- **Wholesale customers** — independent restaurants, hotels, and small offices in Melbourne (5–50 staff)
- **Coffee-curious gift buyers** — visiting Melbourne; buy a bag for a friend back home

### Who we are NOT for
- **Speciality-coffee tourists** seeking the latest experimental processing or rare microlot — Crema is craft, not novelty
- **Convenience-seekers** wanting drive-through or 90-second espresso — we won't install drive-through
- **Bulk corporate accounts** wanting white-label coffee — we only sell beans with the producer's name
- **Customers seeking sweet flavoured drinks** — no syrups, no flavoured lattes

## 3. Logo

### 3.1 Variants

| Variant | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Primary horizontal lockup** (mark + wordmark) | Default. Storefront signage, business cards, hero images |
| **Stacked lockup** (mark above wordmark) | Square contexts, social profile photos |
| **Mark only** (the simplified C ring) | Cup sleeves, app icons, contexts < 50px |
| **Wordmark only** (lowercase "crema" in serif) | Print headers, document footers, contexts where the mark is redundant |
| **Single-colour black on cream** | Print, single-ink reproduction, newspaper |
| **Reverse (cream on Espresso Brown)** | Dark backgrounds, dark mode |
| **App icon** (mark in cream square) | iOS, Android |

### 3.2 Clear Space

Maintain clear space equal to **1× the height of the lowercase 'c'** in the wordmark on all sides. No element — including other logos, photographs, or text — may enter this zone.

### 3.3 Minimum Size

| Context | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Web (mark only) | 24×24px |
| Web (full lockup) | 140px width |
| Print (full lockup) | 30mm width |
| Print (mark only) | 10mm |
| Cup sleeve | 18mm width |

### 3.4 Approved Backgrounds

| Background | Logo variant |
|---|---|
| Cream `#F5EBD8` (Crema House Cream) | Primary (Espresso Brown) |
| White | Primary (Espresso Brown) |
| Sand `#E8D8B8` | Primary (Espresso Brown) |
| Espresso Brown `#3A2A1F` | Reverse (Cream) |
| Photography (with overlay) | Reverse (Cream), with 30% black overlay below |

### 3.5 Misuse — Do Not

1. **Don't stretch or distort.** The lockup proportions are fixed.
2. **Don't change the colours** — Cream and Espresso Brown only.
3. **Don't add effects** — no drop shadows, gradients, glow, or 3D treatment.
4. **Don't rotate** the logo. Ever.
5. **Don't outline** the wordmark or mark.
6. **Don't recreate** from memory — always use the official files.
7. **Don't place on busy backgrounds** without the 30% black overlay.
8. **Don't combine with other logos** in lockups (e.g. wholesale partner co-branding) without brand owner approval.
9. **Don't change the typeface** of the wordmark — it's a custom modification of Domaine Display.
10. **Don't use the mark and wordmark separately on the same surface** — pick one variant per surface.

### 4.3 Approved Combinations

| Foreground | Background | Contrast | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Espresso Brown | Cream | 12.4:1 | Body text on most surfaces |
| Espresso Brown | white | 13.8:1 | Body text on print |
| Cream | Espresso Brown | 12.4:1 | Reverse: text on dark hero |
| Espresso Brown | Sand | 9.2:1 | Accent text |

### 4.4 Forbidden Combinations

- **Sand on Cream** — contrast fails (1.4:1). Sand is a background only, never a text colour on cream.
- **Olive on Cream** — contrast borderline. Use Olive only as a button background or icon, never as text on cream.
- **Roast Amber as text** — fails AA on cream. Use only as a background or large display element.

### 4.5 Accessibility Commitment

All body text in the Crema marketing site and digital wholesale portal passes **WCAG 2.2 AAA** (contrast ratio ≥ 7:1). Validated quarterly via the `contrast-checker.py` script.

## 5. Typography

### 5.1 Type Families

| Role | Typeface | Fallback | License |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display / Heading | Domaine Display | Georgia, serif | Klim Type Foundry — commercial license held |
| Body | Söhne | system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif | Klim Type Foundry — commercial license held |
| Mono (rare) | JetBrains Mono | ui-monospace, monospace | OFL |

### 5.2 Type Scale

| Token | Size | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `text-xs` | 12px | Bag ingredient lists, footnotes |
| `text-sm` | 14px | Captions, secondary metadata |
| `text-base` | 16px | Body text |
| `text-lg` | 18px | Lead body, paragraph intros |
| `text-xl` | 20px | Subheadings on long pages |
| `text-2xl` | 24px | h3 |
| `text-3xl` | 32px | h2 |
| `text-4xl` | 44px | h1 |
| `text-display` | clamp(48px, 4vw + 1rem, 72px) | Hero pages, signage |

### 5.3 Hierarchy Rules

- **Display:** Hero pages and large signage only. Always Domaine Display.
- **h1:** Page title. Domaine Display, weight 400.
- **h2:** Section heading. Domaine Display, weight 400.
- **h3:** Subheading. Söhne Semibold, no italics.
- **Body:** Söhne Regular. Always 16px minimum on web; 11pt minimum in print.
- **Captions:** Söhne Regular at 14px.

### 5.4 Legibility Minimums

- Minimum body text (web): **16px**
- Minimum body text (print): **11pt** (cup sleeves are exempt — minimum 8pt)
- Maximum line length: **70 characters** (about 580px at 16px Söhne Regular)
- Body line height: **1.55**
- Heading line height: **1.15**

### 5.5 Forbidden Type Usage

- **Don't** set body text in Domaine Display. It is a display face.
- **Don't** justify body text — always left-aligned.
- **Don't** use italics for emphasis in headings (Domaine Display has italics but they're decorative — use them sparingly in body, never in headings).
- **Don't** track headings tighter than -1% or wider than +2%.
- **Don't** use system fonts unless the official font has failed to load (fallback chain only).
- **Don't** use Comic Sans or any chalkboard / handwritten font on signage. Crema is craft, not "rustic café."

## 6. Imagery and Iconography

### 6.1 Photography Style

**Direction:** Documentary, not commercial. Shot on real days in real shops, with real customers and real staff. Natural light. Slight underexposure preferred over flash. No filters, no Instagram aesthetics, no corporate stock.

**Subject matter:**
- ✓ Real Crema staff working at the bar
- ✓ Real customers (with consent) drinking coffee in the shop
- ✓ Bean producers in their growing regions (with their permission)
- ✓ Coffee in the cup, naturally lit
- ✓ Quiet shop interiors, neutral and warm
- ✗ Stock photography of any kind
- ✗ Posed "team smiling at the camera" shots
- ✗ Latte art tutorials or competition photos
- ✗ Any photo with the text "good vibes only" or similar
- ✗ Coffee equipment shots without context (no isolated machine porn)

**Treatment:**
- Slight desaturation OK
- Warm colour grading OK (slight)
- No oversaturated colour grading
- No HDR
- No Instagram filters
- No before/after splits

### 6.2 Illustration Style

**Direction:** Hand-drawn line illustrations of producers, growing regions, and coffee plants. Used sparingly — primarily on bean bag stories and special-edition newsletters.

**Style:** Single-line ink, minimal shading, in Espresso Brown on Cream. Never in colour.

**When to use:** When photography of a producer is unavailable, or when marketing a single-origin bag in a story-driven way. Don't use illustration for staff or customers — use real photography.

### 6.3 Iconography

**Set:** Phosphor Icons (regular weight)
**Stroke:** 1.5px
**Style:** Outlined only — never filled
**Usage rules:**
- Always pair an icon with a text label in the website navigation
- Icon-only usage is acceptable in compact UI (e.g. social icons in the footer)
- Icon size: 16px or 20px in body, 24px in headers, 32px in feature callouts
- Icon colour: matches the text colour (Espresso Brown on Cream)

**Forbidden:**
- Filled icons
- Gradient icons
- 3D icons
- Custom icons that don't match Phosphor's stroke conventions
- Coffee-cup or coffee-bean icons (we are Crema; we don't need to telegraph "coffee" with an icon)

### 6.4 Data Visualisation

Wholesale and roastery dashboards use these chart conventions:

- **Default chart palette:** Espresso → Olive → Sand → Roast Amber (in that order)
- **Background:** Always Cream
- **Type in charts:** Söhne Regular at 12px minimum
- **Forbidden:** Pie charts with > 5 slices; 3D charts; rainbow gradients

## 7. Voice and Messaging

### 7.1 Voice Attributes

#### Knowledgeable — this, not lecture-y
We know our craft and we share it freely, but we never make customers feel stupid for not knowing.
- **DO:** "This bean is from a single farm in Yirgacheffe, Ethiopia. The producer's name is Aman Adinew. He grows it at 1,950m above sea level — that altitude is what gives it the bright lemon flavour."
- **DON'T:** "This rare Ethiopian microlot was processed using a heritage natural-anaerobic method, exhibiting tasting notes of citric acid, jasmine, and bergamot. Customers unfamiliar with third-wave coffee may find this challenging."

#### Warm — this, not chummy
We treat customers like neighbours we know, not buddies we're trying too hard to befriend.
- **DO:** "We're here from 7. If you've got a minute, come in early — the first batch of the day is the best one."
- **DON'T:** "Hey friend! 🌟 Pop in for your morning fix and let's caffeinate together!! ☕✨"

#### Considered — this, not stiff
We take time to think before we say things. We never use marketing speak, but we also never undersell what we do.
- **DO:** "This is the only single-origin Yirgacheffe we'll have until next harvest. We bought 60kg. When it's gone, it's gone."
- **DON'T:** "Limited stock! Get yours before it's gone! 🔥"

#### Honest — this, not preachy
We say what's true, including when it's inconvenient. We don't make claims we can't back up.
- **DO:** "We're not certified organic. The producers we work with grow without synthetic chemicals, but the certification process is unaffordable for small farms in Ethiopia. We trust our relationships, not the certification."
- **DON'T:** "100% sustainably sourced ethical coffee from happy farmers! 🌿"

### 7.2 Tone Sliders

| Dimension | Default | Flex when |
|---|---|---|
| Formality | 40% (slightly casual) | Wholesale invoices: 70% formal |
| Energy | 25% (firmly calm) | New shop opening: 40% (still calm) |
| Humour | 25% (mostly serious) | Cup sleeve notes: 50% (occasional dry humour) |
| Warmth | 70% (warm) | Email refunds & complaints: 80% (extra warm) |

### 7.3 Vocabulary

**Approved phrases:**
- "the way we'd make one for a friend"
- "the producer's name is..."
- "boring consistency"
- "slowly, accurately"
- "we trust our relationships"

**Brand-specific terms:**
- **Crema** — always lowercase in body copy, capitalised only at the start of a sentence or in headings. Never CREMA in all caps.
- **Yirgacheffe**, **Sidamo**, **Antigua** — single-origin region names, always capitalised, italicised on first mention only.

### 7.4 Banned Words

We never use:
- Awesome
- Game-changer
- Revolutionary
- World-class
- Best-in-class
- Premium (we are premium; we don't need to say it)
- Artisan / artisanal (overused; meaningless)
- Bespoke
- Curated
- Crafted (cliché in coffee — we are craft, but we don't need to say it)
- Journey ("the coffee journey", "the brewing journey" — banned)
- Vibes / good vibes
- Synergy / leverage / disrupt
- 🔥 emoji in marketing copy
- "Drop" (as a verb meaning "release")

### 7.5 Capitalisation and Punctuation

- **Brand name:** lowercase "crema" in body copy; capitalised "Crema" at start of sentences and in headings
- **Product names:** Title Case (e.g. "House Blend Espresso", "Yirgacheffe Single-Origin")
- **Headings:** Sentence case ("Our latest harvest is in" — not "Our Latest Harvest Is In")
- **Oxford comma:** Yes
- **Em dash:** With spaces, not without ("slow — and honest", not "slow—and honest")
- **Numbers:** Spell out 0–9 in body copy ("seven shops"); use figures from 10 onwards
- **Times:** "7am", not "7:00 AM"
- **Dates:** DD/MM/YYYY ("11/04/2026")

### 7.6 Tagline and Key Messages

**Primary tagline:** "Made the way we'd make one for a friend."

**Elevator pitch (one sentence):** Crema is a Melbourne coffee chain that trains every barista for six weeks before they pull shots, buys beans only from named producers, and refuses to install a robotic bar.

**Elevator pitch (30 seconds):** Crema is a seven-shop coffee chain in Melbourne built on one rule: every coffee should be the way we'd make one for a friend at our own kitchen table. We do that by training every barista for six weeks, buying single-producer beans (always with the producer's name on the bag), and refusing to install a robotic bar — because coffee made by humans takes the time it takes. Boring consistency is the goal. Surprise is for tasting flights, not your morning flat white.

**Key product benefits:**
1. **Consistency you can rely on** — your daily coffee is exactly the same every day
2. **Beans from named producers** — every bag tells you who grew it and where
3. **Trained baristas** — 6-week apprenticeship before they touch a paying customer's coffee

### 7.7 Voice in Context

#### Cup sleeve note (UI microcopy)
> Slowly, accurately. Yirgacheffe today.

#### Marketing landing page (hero)
> # Made the way we'd make one for a friend.
> Seven Melbourne shops. Single-producer beans with the producer's name on the bag. Baristas who apprenticed for six weeks. Boringly consistent on purpose.
> [Find your nearest Crema]

#### Customer support email

## 8. Application Examples

### 8.1 Landing page hero

**Right way:**
- Background: Cream
- Headline: "Made the way we'd make one for a friend." (Domaine Display, 56px)
- Subhead: 18px Söhne Regular, 2 lines max
- CTA: Espresso Brown pill button, "Find your nearest Crema"
- Hero image: documentary photo of staff at the bar (real shop, natural light)

**Wrong way:**
- Background: gradient sand-to-cream (no — gradients are forbidden)
- Headline: "AWESOME COFFEE FOR AWESOME PEOPLE!" (no — banned words, no caps)
- CTA: Roast Amber button (no — fails contrast)

### 8.2 Social media post (Instagram)

**Right way:**
- Image: documentary natural-light photo of a single coffee being made
- Caption: 1–2 sentences in Crema voice, no hashtag spam, no emojis except where natural
- No filter on the image

**Wrong way:**
- Image: stock photo of a generic latte art heart
- Caption: "Good vibes only ☕✨ Drop into Crema today! 🔥 #coffeelover #melbournecoffee #goodvibes"
- VSCO filter applied

### 8.3 Email signature

**Right way:**
```
Maya Tran
Owner, Crema
maya@crema.coffee  ·  +61 ...
crema.coffee
```
- Söhne Regular, 14px, Espresso Brown on white

**Wrong way:**
- Coloured banner image
- Quotes ("Live, laugh, espresso!")
- Multiple social icons
- Logo embedded as image (use the wordmark in text only)

### 8.4 Wholesale bag label

**Right way:**
- Background: Cream
- Mark: top-centre, 15mm wide
- Producer name: prominent, Söhne Semibold, "Aman Adinew"
- Region: Söhne Regular, "Yirgacheffe, Ethiopia"
- Tasting notes: max 3 words, lowercase ("lemon, jasmine, honey")
- Roast date: visible, DD/MM/YYYY format
- No marketing copy on the bag — the bag is the story

**Wrong way:**
- Marketing claims ("award-winning", "world's best")
- Rainbow gradient label
- Latte art photo
- Generic "single origin" without producer name

### 8.5 Café menu board

**Right way:**
- Background: Espresso Brown wall
- Text: Cream Söhne Regular, 60pt minimum (legibility from across the shop)
- Headings: Cream Domaine Display
- No prices in red
- No "specials!" callouts
- No emoji or icons next to drink names

**Wrong way:**
- Chalkboard handwriting
- Coloured prices
- "Today's special!" tags
- Heart icons on lattes
- Drink names in all-caps comic sans
