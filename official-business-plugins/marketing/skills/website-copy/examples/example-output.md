## 2. About

**URL slug:** /about
**Conversion goal:** Build trust → drive to free trial

### Hero

**Headline:** We built the meditation app we wanted.

**Subhead:** Stillwater started in 2023 because none of the existing apps were quiet enough.

### Origin story

In 2022, Anna Rinaldi was a clinical psychologist in Brisbane recommending mindfulness apps to her clients. She kept hearing the same complaint: "I tried it but it felt like another thing I was failing at."

Anna had been meditating for 16 years. She knew that meditation, when it works, is the opposite of a habit-tracking gamified app. It's a quiet thing you do because you've decided to. Streaks, notifications, and "your mood today" prompts undermine the entire practice.

So in late 2022, Anna and her partner Jordan (a designer who'd previously built apps for two of the meditation companies Anna was complaining about) sat down and asked: what would the opposite app look like?

The opposite app:
- Has no streak
- Has no notifications
- Has no AI
- Has no social features
- Has no profile
- Has 240+ practices and one play button

They built it in their kitchen over six months. The first version had three teachers and 28 practices. Today it has 14 teachers and 240+ practices, and we still have the same number of in-app upsells we started with: zero.

### Mission and beliefs

Our mission is straightforward: help people build a meditation practice that survives without our app. The metric we're optimising for is not engagement or revenue per user — it's the percentage of practitioners who, after a year of using Stillwater, continue meditating whether the app is open or closed.

We believe:

- **Meditation is a private practice, not a content product.** We will never have a newsfeed, a community feature, or "shareable progress."
- **The practice is the reward.** No streaks, no badges, no daily challenges, no notifications.
- **Teachers should be paid fairly.** We pay our 14 teachers a flat fee that reflects the work, not a per-stream royalty that incentivises content factory output.
- **Honest is more important than uplifting.** Meditation will not heal your trauma, fix your relationship, or make you wealthy. It might make you a little less reactive over time. That's enough.
- **Less is the feature.** We measure the success of every release by what we removed.

### People

#### Anna Rinaldi, co-founder and head of practice
Anna is a clinical psychologist with 16 years of meditation practice across Buddhist and secular traditions. She trained at the Insight Meditation Society in Massachusetts and has taught beginner meditation classes at the Brisbane Buddhist Centre since 2017. She holds a Doctor of Clinical Psychology from the University of Queensland.

#### Jordan Pak, co-founder and head of design
Jordan previously led design at two of the major meditation apps Anna and her clients were unhappy with. After his second exit, he reached out to Anna and asked if she wanted to build something quieter. He's been meditating since 2018, taught by Anna.

### What we're not

We are not a wellness brand. We don't sell supplements, eye masks, or "ritual kits." We are not a content company; we are not building a publishing arm. We are not an AI company; we will never have an in-app chatbot. We are not for people who want to "transform their life" — we are for people who want to be a little less busy in their own heads.

**Headline:** One screen. One button. Twelve minutes.

**Subhead:** Stillwater is the simplest meditation app you'll use. That is the entire feature list.

### The library

**240+ practices from 14 teachers**

Stillwater has practices in four traditions: Buddhist (Theravada and Tibetan), secular mindfulness, somatic / body-based, and Zen. Sessions range from 8 to 35 minutes. Most practitioners settle on a daily 12-minute session within their first month.

We don't add new practices every week. We add them when a teacher we trust has something worth recording. In 2025 we added 28 practices. In 2026 we expect to add 30–40.

Every teacher is named, has a bio, and is paid a flat fee per recording.

### The screen

**One screen, one button**

When you open Stillwater, you see one screen: a list of your most recent practices and a single play button. There is no:

- Newsfeed
- Profile
- Settings page (well, there's one — for sound output and download quality)
- Streak counter
- Badge or achievement
- Daily challenge
- "Today's intention" prompt
- AI suggestion engine
- Community feature

If you want to find a specific practice, there's a Library tab. That's the second tab. There is no third tab.

### The notifications

**Zero notifications. Ever.**

We do not send push notifications. We do not send daily reminders. We do not send weekly summaries. We do not email you about features. The only email you will ever receive from Stillwater is a billing receipt and an annual reminder before your subscription renews — required by Australian consumer law.

If you don't open the app for a month, we will never know, and we will never ask.

### The cost

**$8/month or $72/year (AUD).** No tiers, no upsells, no premium teacher unlocks, no add-ons. Everything in the library is included.

### Outcome section

**Headline:** What you actually get.

A meditation practice that survives without us. After a year of using Stillwater, most practitioners report sitting whether they remember to open the app or not. We're not trying to be your habit. We're trying to help you build one.

**Why is there only one pricing option?**
Because tiered pricing would incentivise us to lock content behind a higher tier. We're not interested in that. The library is the library; the price is the price.

If you've paid for a year and the app didn't work for you, email us within the first 30 days and we'll refund the year, no questions. After 30 days we don't refund — but you can cancel anytime so the next renewal won't go through.

**Is there a family plan?**
No. Stillwater is a single-player tool by design. If multiple people in a household want to use it, they can each have their own account at the regular price.

## 6. FAQ

### Pricing

**How much does Stillwater cost?**
$8/month or $72/year (AUD). No tiers. No upsells. The 14-day trial is free with no credit card required.

**Is there a free version?**
No permanent free tier — only the 14-day trial. We pay our teachers flat fees and a free tier wouldn't be sustainable.

### The product

**Will you add a streak feature?**
No. Streaks are the opposite of what meditation is for. We will never add a streak counter, badge, or progress tracker.

**Will you add notifications?**
No. We do not send push notifications, daily reminders, or "your friend just meditated" alerts. The only notifications you'll receive from Stillwater are billing-related, as required by AU consumer law.

**Will you add an AI meditation coach?**
No. We are not building an AI feature. The teachers in our library are real humans paid for real recordings.

**Why don't you have a community feature?**
Meditation is a private practice. A "community" feature inside a meditation app is the kind of feature that tests well in usability sessions and undermines the entire practice. We won't build it.

### The library

**Who are your teachers?**
We have 14 teachers across Buddhist (Theravada and Tibetan), secular mindfulness, somatic, and Zen traditions. Each teacher has a bio in the Library tab.

**Do you add new practices regularly?**
We add practices when a teacher we trust has something worth recording — typically 25–40 per year. We don't have a content factory.

**Can I download practices for offline use?**
Yes. Every practice can be downloaded for offline listening. This works on iOS and Android.

### The team

**Who built Stillwater?**
Anna Rinaldi (clinical psychologist, 16-year meditator) and Jordan Pak (designer, formerly at two large meditation apps). The team is now four people. We work from home.
