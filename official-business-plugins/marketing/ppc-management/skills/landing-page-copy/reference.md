# Landing Page Copy — Reference

## 1. Page structures by funnel stage

### Short LP (300–600 words) — cold, low-price / impulse

```
Hero                (one-line promise + CTA)
  ↓
3 value props       (benefit | benefit | benefit)
  ↓
2 testimonials
  ↓
Final CTA
```

### Medium LP (600–1200 words) — mid-price / considered

```
Hero
  ↓
5 value props
  ↓
4 testimonials + trust badges
  ↓
3 objection handlings
  ↓
Pricing
  ↓
FAQ (5)
  ↓
Final CTA
```

### Long LP (1200–2500 words) — high-ticket / complex

```
Hero
  ↓
Problem agitation (2 paras)
  ↓
Promise / solution (1 para)
  ↓
Value props (5–8)
  ↓
Social proof (multiple testimonials + case studies)
  ↓
How it works (process diagram)
  ↓
Objections (6–8)
  ↓
Pricing tiers
  ↓
Deeper FAQ (10–12)
  ↓
Final CTA with urgency
```

## 2. Hero headline formulas

- **Outcome-focused:** "[Desired outcome] without [common friction]" — "Cosy winters without wool that pills"
- **Audience-specific:** "[Product] for [specific audience]" — "Wool throws for Australian winters"
- **Promise:** "[Specific benefit] in [timeframe]" — "Handmade merino throws, delivered in 48 hours"
- **Contrarian:** "[Unexpected claim]" — "The throw you'll still use in 10 years"
- **Story hook:** "[Character] discovered [solution]" — "How 10,000 Aussie homes stayed warm this winter"

## 3. Value proposition formulas

Each VP is: headline (5 words max) + 1-2 sentence explanation + supporting fact.

Examples:

- **Handmade in Australia**
  Every throw is made by hand by a small team in Marrickville. You're supporting 12 Australian makers, not a factory.

- **Built to last**
  Merino wool softens with every wash, not down. These throws get better, not worse.

- **Free shipping over $150**
  No surprises at checkout. Delivery in 2–3 days to every AU metro.

## 4. Social proof types

| Type | When to use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Customer testimonial | Always (best type) | "I've had my Koala throw for 3 years. Still perfect." — Maria, Sydney |
| Star rating | Retail | "⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4.9/5 from 2,300 reviews" |
| Customer count | Scale implied | "10,000+ Australian homes" |
| Press mention | High-trust | "As featured in Vogue Living" |
| Certification | Credentialed | "Responsible Wool Standard certified" |
| Press quote | Authority | "The warmest throw we tested." — Home Beautiful |
| Founder story | Handmade / craft | "Made in Marrickville by a team of 12 Aussie weavers" |

## 5. Objection handling library

Common objections + how to answer:

| Objection | Response pattern |
|---|---|
| "Too expensive" | "Compare the cost per winter. This throw lasts 5+ winters at $26/winter." |
| "Looks the same as other throws" | "3 differences: [1] [2] [3]" with specifics |
| "I'll buy later" | "[Season ending] / [stock limited] without fake urgency" |
| "I don't need this" | Reframe the benefit ("not just warmth — it's the pillow-to-couch everyday staple") |
| "Shipping cost / time" | "Free shipping over $150. Delivered in 2–3 business days." |
| "Return policy" | "30-day returns. No questions. Prepaid return label." |
| "I buy from someone else" | Don't mention competitors. Emphasise the unique benefit. |

## 7. Mobile-first copy rules

- **Paragraphs:** max 3 sentences each.
- **Sentences:** max 25 words.
- **Headers:** frequent — every 3–5 paragraphs.
- **Lists:** use bullets for 3+ items.
- **Bold:** one key phrase per paragraph, not more.
- **Body font size:** assume 16 px on mobile; 12 px is unreadable.
- **CTA position:** above the fold AND at the bottom of every section.
