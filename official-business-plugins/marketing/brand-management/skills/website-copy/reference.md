## 1. Copy Frameworks

### 1.1 AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)

The classic. Used for landing pages and ad copy.

- **Attention:** Headline that stops the scroll
- **Interest:** Subhead that promises a benefit
- **Desire:** Body that paints the outcome
- **Action:** CTA

**When to use:** Cold-traffic landing pages, ads, hero sections.

**Example:**
- Attention: "Stop losing Saturdays to invoice chasing."
- Interest: "Ledgerly puts every overdue payment on one page so you never have to dig through email again."
- Desire: "Spend Sunday with your family instead of sending follow-up reminders. Used by 1,800+ AU SMEs."
- Action: "Try free for 30 days"

### 1.2 PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)

Lean variant of AIDA. Used for pain-driven products.

- **Problem:** Name the problem the customer is having
- **Agitate:** Make the problem feel urgent or bigger than they realised
- **Solution:** Position the brand as the answer

**When to use:** Products that solve acute pain points; content marketing; ad copy.

**Example:**
- Problem: "Your bookkeeper just quit, and you have no idea where the books are."
- Agitate: "BAS is in three weeks. Your accountant is on holiday. The receipts are in 14 different inboxes."
- Solution: "Crema's transition service migrates your books in 5 days flat. Fixed price. No surprises."

### 1.3 BAB (Before → After → Bridge)

The transformation framework. Used for outcome-driven products.

- **Before:** What life looks like with the problem
- **After:** What life looks like solved
- **Bridge:** How the brand gets you from before to after

**When to use:** Lifestyle products, transformative services, products where the outcome is the main story.

**Example:**
- Before: "Your morning starts with 8 unread invoices and 3 reminder emails to write."
- After: "Your morning starts with one notification: 'Everything's on track. Have a coffee.'"
- Bridge: "Ledgerly automates the chase, the reconciliation, and the reminder. You just check that it's done."

### 1.4 StoryBrand (BrandScript)

Donald Miller's framework. Casts the customer as the hero and the brand as the guide.

Components:
1. **Character:** A character with a problem (the customer)
2. **Has a problem:** Both external (the practical issue) and internal (how it makes them feel)
3. **Meets a guide:** Who shows empathy and authority (the brand)
4. **Who gives them a plan:** A clear path forward
5. **And calls them to action:** A direct CTA
6. **That ends in success or failure:** What's at stake

**When to use:** Service businesses, brand-driven sites, B2B with relationship-based sales.

**Example structure for a homepage:**
- Character: "You run a small business."
- Problem (external): "You're drowning in paperwork."
- Problem (internal): "And you feel like you're failing at the job you love."
- Guide: "We've helped 1,800 businesses just like yours."
- Plan: "Three steps: book a call, we set you up, you stop chasing invoices."
- CTA: "Book a free 30-min call"
- Success: "Get your weekends back."
- Failure (implied): "Or keep losing Saturdays."

### 1.5 The Wynter framework (B2B-specific)

For B2B SaaS landing pages. Ordered sections:

1. Brand promise headline
2. Brand promise subhead
3. Hero CTA
4. Trust section (logos, ratings)
5. Problem agitation
6. Product overview
7. How it works (3 steps)
8. Benefits (with proof)
9. Pricing snapshot
10. Final CTA

This framework is opinionated and tested — used by Wynter, Userpilot, and dozens of B2B SaaS landing pages that test well in conversion research.

## 2. Homepage Anatomy

The standard homepage section sequence and what each section needs to accomplish:

### Hero (above the fold)

**Goal:** Make the visitor stay long enough to scroll.

**Required:**
- **Headline** (≤12 words). The biggest single lever in the entire site. Should answer "what is this and is it for me?"
- **Subhead** (≤25 words). Adds the specifics. Often the "and how" to the headline's "what."
- **Primary CTA**. The action you want the visitor to take next.

**Optional:**
- Secondary CTA (a less-committal alternative)
- Hero image / illustration / product screenshot
- Trust signal (e.g. "Trusted by 1,800+ AU SMEs")

**Anti-pattern:** Hero copy that tries to explain the product. The hero's job is to get them to the next section, not to teach them everything.

### Social proof bar

**Goal:** Confirm the brand is legitimate within 5 seconds of landing.

**Required:**
- Logo cluster (4–8 customer logos), or
- Star rating from a third-party review site (G2, Trustpilot, Capterra), or
- Headline metric ("Used by 1,800+ businesses across Australia")

**Anti-pattern:** Vague ("Trusted by leading brands worldwide" with no logos).

### Problem section

**Goal:** Make the visitor feel "this brand gets me."

**Format:**
- Headline that names the problem
- Subhead or short body that elaborates
- Optional: a list of "do you struggle with..." pain points

**Length:** 30–80 words.

### Solution section

**Goal:** Position the brand as the answer to the problem just named.

**Format:**
- Headline that frames the brand's approach
- Body that explains how it works at a high level
- Optional: a short list of solution features

**Length:** 50–120 words.

### Features highlight

**Goal:** Show what the product does without the visitor needing to click to a separate features page.

**Format:**
- 3–4 feature cards
- Each card: icon + feature name + 1-line benefit
- Each card 30–50 words

**Anti-pattern:** Listing 12 features. The features section is a teaser, not a comprehensive list.

### Differentiator section

**Goal:** Answer "why you and not them?"

**Format:**
- Headline naming the differentiator
- Body explaining the contrast
- Optional: comparison table (use sparingly)

**Length:** 80–150 words.

### Social proof (deep)

**Goal:** Provide a moment of "real customer confirmation" before the final CTA.

**Format:**
- 1–3 testimonials with name, role, and (ideally) photo
- Optional: case study highlight

**Anti-pattern:** Anonymous testimonials. "Sarah K., Marketing Manager" is much weaker than "Sarah Kowalski, Marketing Manager at Atlassian."

### Final CTA section

**Goal:** Last-chance conversion before the visitor scrolls past the fold and bounces.

**Format:**
- Headline that restates the value prop
- One CTA, prominent
- Optional: friction-reducer ("No credit card required")

## 3. SEO Meta Best Practices

### Title tag

- **Length:** ≤60 characters (some sources say 55)
- **Format:** {{Page topic}} — {{Brand}} for non-homepage; {{Brand}} — {{value prop}} for homepage
- **Lead with the keyword** if SEO is in scope
- **Brand name at the end**, separated by " — " or " | "
- **Avoid title-stuffing** ("Best, Top, #1, Free" — Google penalises overuse)

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Pricing — Ledgerly" (38 chars)
- ✓ "Ledgerly — Accounting for AU SMEs that just hired a bookkeeper" (62 chars — borderline)
- ✗ "Pricing | Best Accounting Software | Cheap Australian SME Accounting Tool | Ledgerly" (way too long)

### Meta description

- **Length:** ≤155 characters (mobile is shorter — 120 is safer)
- **Format:** A natural sentence (or two) that previews the page
- **Include a CTA** if natural ("Try free for 30 days.")
- **Avoid keyword stuffing**

**Examples:**
- ✓ "See how Ledgerly's pricing works — flat-fee plans for AU SMEs ready to hand off the books. Try free for 30 days." (118 chars)
- ✗ "Best accounting software pricing for cheap small business accounting Australia book SME finance tool." (102 chars but unreadable)

### URL slug

- **Lowercase, hyphenated**
- **≤5 words**
- **Match the page topic**
- **No stop words** (the, of, for) unless necessary

**Examples:**
- ✓ /pricing
- ✓ /about
- ✓ /case-studies/atlassian
- ✗ /our-pricing-page-for-our-amazing-product

### Open Graph image

- **Dimensions:** 1200×630px
- **Show:** Brand logo + page topic + key visual
- **Avoid:** Long text overlays (mobile previews crop badly)

## 4. CTA Verb Library

Strong CTA verbs grouped by intent:

### Trial / Sign-up
- Start free
- Try free for {N} days
- Get started free
- Create your account
- Start your trial

### Book / Schedule
- Book a demo
- Book a 30-min call
- Schedule a chat
- Reserve your slot
- Pick a time

### Buy / Purchase
- Buy now
- Add to cart
- Order now
- Get yours
- Subscribe

### Contact / Lead
- Get in touch
- Contact sales
- Ask a question
- Talk to us
- Send a message

### Learn / Read
- See how it works
- Watch the demo
- Read the case study
- Browse the {N} examples
- Take the tour

### Download
- Download the {asset}
- Get the {asset}
- Send me the {asset}

### Anti-patterns

- **"Click here"** — never. The link text should be the CTA copy.
- **"Submit"** — generic; replace with action-specific verb
- **"Learn more"** — too vague; replace with what they'll learn
- **"Get started"** — what does "started" mean? Be specific.

### Friction-honest verbs

CTAs that set realistic expectations convert better than CTAs that hide friction:

- ✓ "Book a 30-min call" (sets expectation)
- ✗ "Talk to us" (vague — could be a 5-min email or a 2-hour sales call)
- ✓ "Start your 14-day free trial" (sets expectation about duration)
- ✗ "Try it" (hides whether it costs money)

## 5. Length Guidelines

| Element | Word count |
|---|---|
| Headline | 5–12 words |
| Subhead | 12–25 words |
| Section heading | 3–8 words |
| Body section | 60–150 words |
| Feature card | 25–40 words |
| Testimonial | 30–60 words |
| FAQ answer | 30–100 words |
| About-page founder bio | 80–150 words |
| Case study summary | 200–400 words |
| Hero CTA button | 1–6 words |

**General principle:** Cut 30% of every draft. The second draft is always shorter than the first; the third is always shorter than the second.

## 6. Voice Compliance Checks

Before declaring website copy complete, verify against the brand voice rules:

- [ ] No banned words anywhere in the copy
- [ ] Tone is consistent across pages (with deliberate flex per page)
- [ ] Voice attributes (from brand-identity) are visible — at least 2 of the 3–5 attributes show in every page
- [ ] CTAs match the brand voice (a quiet brand doesn't have shouty CTAs)
- [ ] Capitalisation conventions followed (brand name, product names, headings)
- [ ] Banned punctuation patterns avoided (excess exclamation marks, emoji in marketing copy, em dash style)
- [ ] Anti-brand respected (no copy that does what the brand explicitly doesn't do)
- [ ] Persona is addressed (no generic "you, the customer" — speak to a specific person)

## 7. Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Writing about the brand
"We are a leading provider of innovative solutions" — this is about the brand, not the customer. Rewrite to be about the customer's outcome.

### Mistake 2: Listing features without benefits
"Real-time sync, multi-device support, end-to-end encryption" — these are features. The benefit is what the customer gets *because of* the features.

### Mistake 3: Vague social proof
"Trusted by leading brands" — meaningless. Name the brands. Show the logos.

### Mistake 4: Multiple primary CTAs
Two equally-prominent CTAs on the hero confuses visitors. Pick one primary; make any other CTA visibly secondary.

### Mistake 5: Pricing page that hides pricing
"Contact us for pricing" makes evaluators leave. If the brand really doesn't want public pricing, the pricing page becomes a "how pricing works" page that explains the engagement model.

### Mistake 6: Long FAQ that buries the answers
A 30-question FAQ is unread. Pick the 8–12 questions that matter most and answer them fully.

### Mistake 7: Hero copy that explains the product
The hero's job is to get visitors to the next section. Save the explanation for the features section.

## 8. Output Validation Checklist

Before declaring the website copy complete:

- [ ] Every page in the sitemap has full copy (no placeholders)
- [ ] Every page has SEO meta (title ≤60 chars, description ≤155 chars, slug)
- [ ] Every page has a primary CTA
- [ ] Every CTA is verb-led and specific
- [ ] No banned words anywhere
- [ ] Voice compliance check passed
- [ ] At least one testimonial with named attribution somewhere on the site
- [ ] Pricing page has clear next steps even if pricing is on request
- [ ] Footer copy includes legal links (privacy, terms) — even if those pages aren't generated by this skill
- [ ] Open Graph image descriptions provided for each page
