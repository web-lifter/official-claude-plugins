## Primary sources

- **McClure, Dave.** "Startup Metrics for Pirates: AARRR!" (500 Startups, 2007.) <https://500hats.typepad.com/500blogs/2007/09/startup-metrics.html>
  - Origin of the AARRR funnel (Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral).
  - The 5-stage funnel used in this skill is a venture-stage adaptation: Awareness → Sign-up → Activation → Conversion → Retention. The mapping is straightforward (McClure's Acquisition splits into Awareness+Sign-up; Revenue collapses into Conversion; Referral is handled separately by `get-keep-grow-design`).
- **Skok, David.** "SaaS Metrics 2.0" (For Entrepreneurs, 2013). <https://www.forentrepreneurs.com/saas-metrics-2/>
  - Canonical SaaS-funnel benchmarks for conversion-rate sanity-checks.
- **Croll, Alistair & Yoskovitz, Ben.** *Lean Analytics.* O'Reilly, 2013.
  - Stage-appropriate funnel metrics — early-stage ventures should over-index on Activation and Retention; mature ventures on Conversion.

## Why the end-to-end yield reality check

From Skok (2013) and McClure (2007), end-to-end funnel yield for cold/warm B2B SaaS outbound typically lands between 0.2% and 1% pre-PMF. Yields outside that band (either direction) signal a model error: too-high suggests rate inflation; too-low suggests a channel-fit problem.

The skill flags yields below 1% as "typical floor for cheap channels" and yields above 30% as "suspiciously high" — both prompts to re-check the rate sources.
