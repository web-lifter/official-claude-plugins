## Primary sources

- **Eberle, Bob.** *SCAMPER: Games for Imagination Development.* Prufrock Press, 1996.
  - The seven-lens divergence prompt set (Substitute / Combine / Adapt / Modify / Put-to-another-use / Eliminate / Reverse). Maps onto the Inverse, Constraint-relaxing, Constraint-tightening, and Cross-domain categories used here.
- **IDEO.** *The Field Guide to Human-Centered Design.* IDEO.org, 2015.
  - Defines the diverge/converge double-diamond and the "defer judgement, go for quantity" rules baked into this skill's principles.
- **Brown, Tim.** *Change by Design.* HarperBusiness, 2009.
  - IDEO CEO's canonical treatment of design thinking as a method, including the case for cross-domain analogies.

## Why the six categories

The Direct / Inverse / Constraint-relaxing / Constraint-tightening / Cross-domain / Strange split is a SCAMPER-derived structure tuned for product-shape ideation specifically (vs. SCAMPER's broader product-feature focus). It enforces that the strange/cross-domain categories — where the novel ideas usually live — are not skipped.

## Anti-patterns (cited in SKILL.md)

- Critiquing during divergence kills it (IDEO; Brown).
- Stopping at the first 5 obvious ideas misses the long-tail novelty (Eberle).
