# divergent-ideate — reference

## §1. Generation prompts by category

### Direct solutions
- "What's the most obvious thing that addresses <problem>?"
- "If we just took the value-map's top reliever and built it minimally,
  what would that look like?"

### Inverse solutions
- "What's the *opposite* of what competitors do?"
- "What if the customer never came back? What product makes that
  okay?"
- "What if our segment paid us *not* to use it?"

### Constraint-relaxing
- "If we had unlimited engineering capacity, what would we build?"
- "If the customer had unlimited budget, what would they buy?"
- "If we had 10 years of customer data, what would we do?"

### Constraint-tightening
- "What can we ship in a weekend?"
- "What can we ship for AUD $0?"
- "What can we ship without writing any code?"
- "What can we ship without any backend?"

### Cross-domain
- "What does <healthcare | airline | luxury | gaming> do for a
  similar problem?"
- "How does <large industry> solve customer onboarding?"

### Strange / playful
- "What's a deliberately silly version of this product?"
- "What if the product were a person?"
- "What if it could only be used at night?"

## §2. Anti-patterns

- Critiquing during generation — the silent killer of divergence
- Stopping at the first 5 obvious ideas — the next 15 are where the
  novelty lives
- Treating direct solutions as the "real" answers and the rest as
  noise — sometimes the strange ones become the wedge

## §3. When to skip the strange category

If the venture is in late discovery (≥ 5 segment interviews, validated
hypothesis) and just looking to flesh out a known good direction,
constrain to Direct + Constraint-tightening categories only. Strange
ideas at that stage usually waste time.

But for early-stage ventures, strange ideas often surface assumptions
worth questioning.
