# Early adopters — AU/NZ mid-market in-house counsel

Two named earlyvangelists at 5/5 criteria with ≥ 2× engagement. We need at least 3 to clear `customer-discovery-status` Q3 — one candidate (P7) is currently 4/5; book a follow-up to verify criterion 3.

## The five criteria for this segment

### 1. Have the problem

They are the sole or near-sole in-house counsel at a 50–500-staff AU/NZ company. The observable signal is a Slack history showing late-night markup of MSAs or a calendar showing post-9pm legal review blocks 2+ times in the last quarter. Per the segment profile, pains P-01 (late-night MSA review), P-02 (missed auto-renewal incident), and P-06 (single-point-of-failure anxiety) all apply.

### 2. Know they have it

They will spontaneously name the problem when asked "what's the hardest part of your week?" without prompting around contracts. They distinguish themselves from counsel who say "it's busy but manageable" — earlyvangelists say things like "I lost a whole Thursday evening to a vendor MSA last week".

### 3. Actively looking for a solution

They've tried at least one of: Spellbook trial, ChatGPT (with concerns), a junior paralegal review pass, or a Word add-in like Litera. Search terms include "AU contract AI review", "Spellbook Australia", "in-house counsel automation". They post in the ACC Australia community and on LinkedIn corporate-counsel groups.

### 4. Cobbled together a workaround

The dominant workaround is a personal Excel spreadsheet of "buyer shall…" clauses, manually maintained per contract. Some use a Word template of common redline language they paste in. P3 (Brisbane edtech) maintains a colour-coded markup convention; P5 (Sydney health-tech) has a paralegal do a first pass on supplier contracts < AU$50k.

### 5. Has budget

The budget sits in the legal department's tool line (~AU$10k–AU$40k per year at this company size) or, more often, in procurement's "supplier-onboarding tooling" line. P3 controls a small discretionary spend (~AU$5k/year); P5 has to get procurement sign-off but it's a known path.
