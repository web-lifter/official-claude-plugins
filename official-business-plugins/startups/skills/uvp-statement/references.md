# references — uvp-statement

The standard UVP template — "For [customer] who [need], our [product] is a [category] that [unique benefit]. Unlike [competitor], we [differentiator]." — is widely associated with:

- **Moore, Geoffrey A.** *Crossing the Chasm.* HarperBusiness, 1991 (revised 2014). — the "high-concept positioning statement" template that originates this form.

The same structure is repeated and refined in:

- **Maurya, Ash.** *Running Lean* (3rd ed.). O'Reilly, 2022. — UVP as a deliberate output of the lean-canvas process.
- **Osterwalder, Alexander; Pigneur, Yves; Bernarda, Greg; Smith, Alan.** *Value Proposition Design.* Wiley, 2014. — the VPC's pain-reliever / gain-creator pairs feed directly into the "unique benefit" slot.

The skill requires that the `differentiator` slot describe something **observable** (e.g. "we cover AU/NZ commercial MSAs and flag PPSA-specific risks") rather than aspirational ("better UX").
