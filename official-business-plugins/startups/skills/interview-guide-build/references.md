# interview-guide-build — references

The interview-guide structure this skill enforces (≤ 30 minutes, open questions, behavioural recall, four mandatory closers) comes from Lean Startup customer-discovery practice. See [`startups/SOURCES.md`](../../../SOURCES.md) for full citations.

## Lean-Startup interview discipline

- **Ries, Eric.** *The Lean Startup.* Crown Business, 2011.
  - Customer-discovery interviews are the lowest-cost validated-learning artefact a venture can produce. Ries treats them as the canonical alternative to "feature factory" guesswork.
- **Maurya, Ash.** *Running Lean* (3rd ed.). O'Reilly, 2022.
  - Practical interview tactics: open questions, behavioural recall ("walk me through the last time…"), no pitching during problem-discovery, the four-closer pattern that turns one interview into a referral chain.

## Why open, behavioural, non-leading

A yes/no question gets a yes/no answer that says nothing useful. "Would you use a tool that does X?" tests the interviewee's politeness, not their behaviour — research repeatedly shows people overstate future intent. "What did you do last time X happened?" tests memory of actual action, which is far more predictive.

This skill rejects and regenerates questions that fail the three tests (open, non-leading, specific). Examples of failure modes live in [`reference.md`](reference.md).

## Why 30 minutes

A 45-minute guide gets cut for time and loses the close. The four closers ("why," "why not," "who else," "can we follow up") are the highest-value part of the conversation — each one alone is worth more than the body of the interview because they generate referrals and follow-ups. Designing for 30 minutes preserves the close.

## Why the solution probe is optional

Pure problem-discovery beats premature solution-validation. The solution probe runs only if the interviewee raises the topic of solutions or asks what we're working on. This protects against the most common interview failure: pitching the product and mistaking polite agreement for evidence.
