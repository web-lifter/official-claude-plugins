# interview-guide-build — reference

## §1. Question quality examples

### Good (open, behavioural, specific)

- "Walk me through the last time you reconciled invoices."
- "When was the most recent time you tried to find a new supplier?
  What did you do?"
- "When this got frustrating, what did you do next?"
- "Who else was involved in the decision?"

### Bad (leading, hypothetical, yes/no)

- "Would you use a tool that does X?" → leading hypothetical, not
  behavioural
- "Don't you find it frustrating that ...?" → leading
- "Are you happy with your current process?" → yes/no
- "Wouldn't it be great if ...?" → leading hypothetical

### Salvage patterns

| Bad | Salvaged |
|---|---|
| "Do you find X frustrating?" | "When did X last bother you? What did you do?" |
| "Would you pay for Y?" | "What have you spent on solving this in the last year?" |
| "Don't you wish you could ...?" | "Walk me through the last time you needed to ..." |

## §2. The four closers (verbatim)

These are the fixed end-of-interview questions. Don't paraphrase.

1. **"Why does this matter to you?"** — surfaces the underlying
   motivation, often emotional, that the body of the interview missed.
2. **"Why haven't you solved this already?"** — exposes the existing
   workaround and the cost of switching.
3. **"Who else should we talk to?"** — referrals are the cheapest
   recruitment channel.
4. **"Can we follow up in a few weeks?"** — signals we want a
   relationship, not a single data point.

## §3. Time budget

| Section | Min | Target | Max |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open | 1 | 2 | 3 |
| Day-in-the-life walk | 5 | 8 | 12 |
| Problem deep-dive | 6 | 10 | 12 |
| Solution probe (optional) | 0 | 4 | 6 |
| Close | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| **Total** | **14** | **27** | **37** |

If the generated guide exceeds 30 minutes max, trim the solution probe
first, then the day-in-the-life walk, never the close.

## §4. Customising for B2B vs B2C

- **B2B**: include a "Who else owns this decision?" question in the
  problem deep-dive. Multiple stakeholders is the norm.
- **B2C**: include a "When you tried, what stopped you?" — friction
  questions matter more than approval-process questions.

## §5. Recording and consent

The guide's "Open" section includes the recording-permission line by
default. If the venture's privacy policy disallows recording (some
clinical / regulated settings), the skill should swap the recording
line for "I'll be taking detailed notes" and surface a warning to the
user.
