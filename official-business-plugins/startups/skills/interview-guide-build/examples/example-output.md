# Interview guide — AU/NZ mid-market in-house counsel

Target time: **30 minutes**. Bias to open questions, behavioural recall, and follow-ups. The four closers at the end are mandatory. Do not pitch ContractIQ unless the interviewee raises the topic of solutions — pure problem-discovery beats premature solution-validation every time.

## Hypotheses in scope

- H-001: AU/NZ mid-market in-house counsel will pay AU$300/seat/month for clause-classification + obligation-extraction
- H-002: A senior in-house counsel produces a redline of a 40-page MSA in < 25 min median using the tool
- H-004: Procurement managers hold the budget and will sponsor pilots even when legal is the user

## 1. Open (≤ 3 min)

- "Thanks for the time. Quick context: I'm Priya — used to be GC at a mid-market AU SaaS, now researching how in-house counsel review commercial contracts. Not selling anything today."
- "Mind if I record? Just so I can re-listen rather than scribble. I'll send you a copy."
- "I'll keep it to 30 — and I'll watch the clock so you don't have to."

## 2. Day-in-the-life walk (≤ 8 min)

- "Walk me through the last commercial MSA you reviewed."
- "How did it land on your desk? What did you do first?"
- "What were you using to mark it up? Who else was involved — paralegal, partnerships, finance?"
- "What time of day did you finish? Was that typical?"
- "What was the most frustrating part of that review?"

## 3. Problem deep-dive (≤ 10 min)

- "When did a missed clause last cost the company money? Walk me through what happened."
- "How do you track obligations once a contract is signed? — can I see what that looks like?"
- "Have you tried any AI tools for review — Spellbook, ChatGPT, anything else? What happened?"
- "What did that cost you — time, money, mood, reputation with the business?"
- "If you could wave a wand and have *one* part of contract review just disappear, what would it be?"

## 4. Solution probe (≤ 6 min, only if appropriate — skip otherwise)

- "If a tool could give you a redline + a one-page negotiation brief in 20 minutes on a 40-page MSA, what would you do with the time you got back?"
- "What would have to be true for you to trust the redline?"
- "Who in your business would need to approve a AU$300/seat/month subscription?"

## 5. Close (≤ 3 min) — the four closers

- Why does this matter to you?
- Why haven't you solved this already?
- Who else should we talk to? (Especially: procurement, finance, the business owner who lives with the contract afterwards.)
- Can we follow up in a few weeks?

## Rubric for capturing

When logging via `/interview-log au-midmarket-inhouse-counsel`, capture for each hypothesis:

- Confirmed | Refuted | Ambiguous — with the verbatim quote or observed behaviour.
- For H-001 willingness to pay: did they spontaneously give a number? Was AU$300/seat/month inside or outside their frame?
- For H-002 redline speed: how long do they say a 40-page MSA review takes them today?
- For H-004 procurement budget: did they name a specific procurement contact and an existing budget line?
