# references — experiment-design

The experiment-type menu — customer interview, survey, smoke test, concierge MVP, Wizard of Oz, fake door, A/B, pre-order, letter of intent — is the standard palette in:

- **Maurya, Ash.** *Running Lean* (3rd ed.). O'Reilly, 2022. — Chapter on validation experiments. Maurya groups experiments by phase (problem, solution, scale) and by what each can falsify.
- **Osterwalder, Alexander; Pigneur, Yves; Bernarda, Greg; Smith, Alan.** *Value Proposition Design.* Wiley, 2014. — the Strategyzer "test card library" provides the canonical templates for each experiment type.
- **Blank, Steve & Dorf, Bob.** *The Startup Owner's Manual.* K&S Ranch, 2012. — customer-development experiments and pass/fail criteria.

The "confidence × cost" recommendation logic prefers the cheapest experiment that can produce a clear answer — Maurya's risk × impact × ease rubric (see `experiment-prioritise/references.md`) applied at the experiment-type granularity rather than the hypothesis granularity.
