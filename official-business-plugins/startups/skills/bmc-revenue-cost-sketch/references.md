## Unit-economics primer

A range-only sketch of the BMC's Revenue Streams and Cost Structure cells is **deliberately pre-rigour**. The intent is to surface assumptions as hypotheses, not to produce a financial model.

Two concepts borrowed from operating-finance practice:

- **Contribution margin** = (price per customer) − (variable cost per customer). For a SaaS subscription this is roughly `MRR − (per-customer compute + per-customer support + payment fees)`.
- **Unit economics** = the per-customer view of profitability over the customer's lifetime — typically expressed as the ratio of **LTV (lifetime value)** to **CAC (customer acquisition cost)**. A widely-cited heuristic is LTV / CAC ≥ 3 for a healthy SaaS business (David Skok, *Matrix Partners* essays on SaaS metrics, 2010s).

When the venture has any of:

- ≥ 1 paid pilot (real revenue numbers)
- ≥ 1 month of infrastructure billing (real cost numbers)
- A go-to-market plan needing CAC / LTV modelling

…escalate to **`business-economics/unit-economics`** for the modelled version. This sketch should be linked from there as the precursor.
