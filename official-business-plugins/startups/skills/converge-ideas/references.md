## Primary sources

- **IDEO.** *The Field Guide to Human-Centered Design.* IDEO.org, 2015.
  - Defines the converge half of the double-diamond. Uses the "impact vs feasibility" 2×2 and dot-voting as the canonical convergence tools.
- **Knapp, Jake; Zeratsky, John; Kowitz, Braden.** *Sprint: How to Solve Big Problems and Test New Ideas in Just Five Days.* Simon & Schuster, 2016.
  - The Google Ventures design-sprint convergence pattern: silent vote → dot-vote → decider. Source of the "three at most" finalist rule.
- **Osterwalder, Pigneur, Bernarda, Smith.** *Value Proposition Design.* Wiley, 2014.
  - Source of the "test card" discipline — every finalist needs a hypothesis it tests.

## The four filters

The Segment-fit / Hypothesis-test-value / Build-cost / Novelty-wedge filter set is a venture-stage adaptation of the IDEO impact-vs-feasibility 2×2 — split into four axes so that build-cost and novelty don't get conflated with impact.

## Why three finalists max

From *Sprint* (Knapp et al., 2016): cognitive load on a small founding team is the constraint. More than three finalists means none get the dedicated prototyping bandwidth they need to produce a credible signal.
