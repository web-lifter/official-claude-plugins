# 1-minute pitch

**Audience:** Investor (angel / pre-seed)
**Generated:** 2026-05-21

## Script (148 words)

### Problem (~15s)
In-house counsel at Australian and New Zealand mid-market companies review eight to twenty-five commercial contracts a week — usually alone, often at 9pm on a Thursday because the business needs sign-off Friday morning. They miss auto-renewal clauses that auto-bill the company eighty thousand dollars eighteen months later, and there is no system to track obligations once a contract is signed.

### Insight (~10s)
Spellbook and LawGeex are tuned for US contract corpora. The AU and NZ market has specific risks — PPSA, Privacy Act schedule 1, Modern Slavery Act reporting — that those tools don't flag. Counsel here have been settling for ChatGPT, against their own confidentiality policies.

### Solution (~15s)
**ContractIQ** is a contract-review co-pilot trained on AU and NZ commercial MSAs. Upload a forty-page contract; in under twenty minutes, counsel get a ranked list of risky clauses, missing protections, and a tracked-changes redline. Built on Next.js, Supabase and Cloudflare.

### Traction / why now (~10s)
Twelve in-house counsel in a paid usability study starting next Monday; three letters of intent at AU$300 per seat per month; Anthropic ZDR addendum signed.

### Ask (~10s)
Two warm intros to AU SaaS angels with legal-tech exposure.
