# Channel strategy

The core trade-off for ContractIQ at this stage: **direct (founder-led sales)** vs **indirect (partner/integration with an existing CLM such as Bigle or Ironclad)**. Direct wins for now because the founder has the legal-network warm intros and pre-revenue economics make partner integration premature.

## Primary: Direct founder-led sales via LinkedIn outbound to AU/NZ in-house counsel

- **Type:** direct
- **Why segment fit:** AU/NZ in-house counsel are highly active on LinkedIn (the de-facto professional registry for the legal community). Priya (founder, ex-GC) has a 1,400-person network, ~280 of whom fit the primary segment.
- **Why product fit:** B2B SaaS at $300/seat/month with a 1-lawyer-team buyer requires consultative selling — a Calendly-and-Zoom motion is the right shape until we have 10+ paying logos. See product-channel-fit-check matrix row "Enterprise SaaS" (mid-market variant).
- **Cost to learn:** ~10 hours/week of Priya's time for 8 weeks. Marginal cost: AU$0 (LinkedIn Sales Navigator already in stack).
- **Cost to scale:** breaks at ~50 logos; that's when we hire an AE or shift to inbound + ABM.
- **Driver hypothesis:** **H-001 (demand)** — at the AU$300/seat price point, ≥ 30% of 20 discovery interviewees express willingness to pay.

## Secondary: ALPMA (Australasian Legal Practice Management Association) annual conference + state branch events

- **Type:** direct (event-based)
- **Why segment fit:** ALPMA gathers the operational legal professionals who set the tooling agenda for AU/NZ legal teams. State-branch events are smaller and cheaper to attend than the national conference.
- **Why product fit:** in-person trust-building is high-leverage for a contract-review tool — GCs need to see a human's face before trusting an AI with their MSAs.
- **Cost to learn:** AU$2,800 conference + travel for 2026 national event.
- **Cost to scale:** poor — events are episodic. Use them for trust, not pipeline volume.
- **Driver hypothesis:** **H-101** (new) — "≥ 5 qualified discovery conversations per state branch event."

## Tertiary: GC peer-network referrals (Aubergine Health → next 3 logos)

- **Type:** direct (referral)
- **Why segment fit:** AU GCs are a small, gossipy community of ~600 practitioners; a single happy customer is worth 3 cold outreaches.
- **Why product fit:** strong — peer endorsement collapses the trust-building cost.
- **Cost to learn:** ~AU$0; structured ask in every Quarterly Business Review.
- **Cost to scale:** caps at one referral every 6–8 weeks per customer. Becomes the primary channel once we have 8+ paying logos.
- **Driver hypothesis:** **H-102** — "≥ 50% of paying customers introduce us to at least 1 peer in the first 90 days."

## Channels we are NOT pursuing (and why)

- **Indirect via Ironclad / LinkSquares / Bigle partnerships.** CLM partnership conversations require us to have a credible product and 5+ logos; we have neither. Revisit at the Series A.
- **Paid social (LinkedIn Ads).** CAC at our price point ≈ AU$2,000+ per qualified lead based on competitor inferences; LTV at AU$3,600/year/seat doesn't support it pre-product-market-fit.
- **Content marketing (SEO long-game).** Worthwhile but takes 12+ months to compound. Defer until after H-001 is validated; revisit as a Keep-stage activity.
- **Conferences outside ALPMA** (Legalwise, Mondaq summits). Sponsor cost too high relative to attendee fit.
- **Procurement-led channels** (procurement.com.au). Secondary segment; not enough leverage yet.
