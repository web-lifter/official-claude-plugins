# six-ways-to-innovate — references

The six lenses this skill applies come from Strategyzer. See [`startups/SOURCES.md`](../../../SOURCES.md) for full citations.

## The six lenses

- **Osterwalder, Alexander; Pigneur, Yves; Bernarda, Greg; Smith, Alan.** *Value Proposition Design.* Wiley, 2014.
  - Source of the six lenses: (1) customer needs, (2) value (price, performance, customisation), (3) the product itself, (4) the experience, (5) the relationship, (6) the channel. Strategyzer's claim is that any value-proposition refinement worth considering will surface under one of these six lenses; running them as a checklist counters the "we just incrementally improve the current product" trap.

## Why candidates, not facts

The output of this skill is generative — a list of *hypotheses worth considering*, not decisions. The user prunes the list and promotes the worthwhile candidates to the hypothesis register via `/hypothesis-register add`, where they acquire a falsifier, measurement, threshold, and timeframe before any test is run. This separation keeps the canvas an evidence-backed artefact even while the brainstorm itself is loose.

## Why dedupe against the register

A candidate that is already a tracked hypothesis is not a new idea; surfacing it twice wastes the user's attention. The skill reads the existing hypothesis register before generating candidates so it can flag the deduplication ("we already track this — see H-NN").

## Six lenses, not five

Each lens produces at least one candidate, even if the answer is "nothing obvious here". The discipline is the checklist itself; skipping a lens because "we don't do partnerships" is the failure mode the lens exists to detect.
