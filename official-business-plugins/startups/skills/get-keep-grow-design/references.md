## Primary sources

- **Osterwalder, Alexander & Pigneur, Yves.** *Business Model Generation.* Wiley, 2010.
  - The Customer Relationships cell of the Business Model Canvas. Defines the lifecycle as Get → Keep → Grow — each with its own activities, metrics, and investments.
  - Source of the "skipping Grow because we'll figure it out later" failure-mode warning.
- **Blank, Steve & Dorf, Bob.** *The Startup Owner's Manual.* K&S Ranch, 2012.
  - Practical operationalisation: each lifecycle stage has its own funnel and its own hypotheses.

## Why three stages, not one

Per Osterwalder & Pigneur (2010): conflating Get with Keep produces vanity-metric Customer Acquisition Cost calculations that ignore the cost of retention. Conflating Keep with Grow produces a "we have happy customers" trap with no expansion plan. The three-stage split forces the venture to plan and instrument each lifecycle phase distinctly.

## Each stage has its own metrics

- **Get:** CAC, MQLs, conversion rate per channel.
- **Keep:** retention rate, NPS, week-N active ratio, churn.
- **Grow:** NRR, expansion MRR, referral rate, seat-attach.

The skill enforces this separation in the output template — don't measure Get with retention metrics.
