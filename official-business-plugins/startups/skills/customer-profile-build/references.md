# customer-profile-build — references

The customer profile (right half of the Value Proposition Canvas) per Strategyzer, with jobs tagged functional/social/emotional per the jobs-to-be-done lens. See [`startups/SOURCES.md`](../../../SOURCES.md) for full citations.

## The Value Proposition Canvas

- **Osterwalder, Alexander; Pigneur, Yves; Bernarda, Greg; Smith, Alan.** *Value Proposition Design.* Wiley, 2014.
  - Canonical reference for the Value Proposition Canvas: customer profile (jobs / pains / gains) on the right, value map (products & services, pain relievers, gain creators) on the left. This skill produces the right half; [`value-proposition-canvas/value-map-build`](../../../value-proposition-canvas/skills/value-map-build/) produces the left.

## Jobs-to-be-done tagging

- **Christensen, Clayton M.; Hall, Taddy; Dillon, Karen; Duncan, David S.** *Competing Against Luck.* Harper Business, 2016.
  - Definitive treatment of the jobs-to-be-done lens. A job is something the customer is *trying to get done*, and it always has a functional dimension, often a social one, and sometimes an emotional one. Strategyzer's profile uses the same three-way tag.

## Why force prioritisation

If every pain is "high" priority, the team has not yet made a strategy decision. The skill refuses uniform-priority profiles for the same reason `hypothesis-register` refuses statements without falsifiers: a profile that has not been *prioritised* cannot drive a `value-map-build` that focuses on the right relievers and creators.

## Why "observable items only"

Both Strategyzer and Christensen ground their lenses in observable behaviour. "Frustrated by paperwork" is a feeling; "Spends 4 hours every Friday on reconciliation" is an observation. The latter can be matched to a pain reliever ("auto-reconciles transactions from the bank feed in real time") with a clear, testable mechanism. The former cannot.
