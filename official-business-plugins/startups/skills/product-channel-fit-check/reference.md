# product-channel-fit-check — reference

## §1. Fit matrix

| Product type | Coherent | Unconventional | Incoherent |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Self-serve SaaS** ($/mo, B2B or B2C) | Direct web, content, freemium funnel, app stores (mobile-first), product-led growth | Inside sales (mid-market), enterprise referrals | Retail, traditional advertising for B2B SaaS |
| **Enterprise SaaS** | Inside sales, channel partners, conferences, exec referrals | Freemium → land-and-expand | App store, free trials with no human contact |
| **Consumer mobile app** | App store optimisation, paid social, influencer, virality, word-of-mouth | Email-first, content | Inside sales, retail |
| **Consumer web app** | Direct web (SEO, SEM), content, paid social, virality | App store (if PWA) | Inside sales, retail |
| **Physical consumer product** | Direct e-commerce, retail, online marketplaces (Amazon, Etsy) | Subscription clubs, direct mail | Inside sales (unless luxury / B2B) |
| **B2B physical product** | Sales reps, distributors, trade shows | Direct e-commerce | App store, paid social |
| **Service / consulting** | Referrals, thought-leadership content, conferences, sales | Productised package on web | Retail, app store |
| **Marketplace / multi-sided** | Each side has its own channel mix; usually direct web for one side and sales/community for the other | One side via partner | Identical channels for both sides usually fail |
| **Open-source + paid tier** | Community, content, OSS distribution, GitHub-led | Inside sales for enterprise tier | Retail |

## §2. Why coherence matters

A SaaS sold via retail forces the customer through a packaging /
licence-key / installation flow that doesn't match the product
expectation. A mass-market consumer app sold by inside sales has
unit economics that can't work — the CAC is wrong by an order of
magnitude.

The matrix is heuristic, not law. The unconventional column lists
known exceptions where the pairing has worked under specific
conditions.

## §3. Common 🔴 patterns

- **B2C SaaS via inside sales** — usually CAC > LTV
- **B2B SaaS via app store** — buyers don't choose tools that way
- **Physical via inside sales (non-luxury)** — margin doesn't support
  it
- **Marketplaces with the same channel for both sides** — usually
  starves one side; tip and unbalanced supply

## §4. When the matrix is wrong

If the venture has compelling evidence that the unconventional pairing
works (a learning card with strong evidence), record it. The matrix
is a starting point, not a verdict. Document the deviation in the
fit report's rationale column.
