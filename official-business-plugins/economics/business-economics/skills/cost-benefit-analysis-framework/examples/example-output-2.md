# Cost-Benefit Analysis — Product Line Expansion vs Existing-Market Deepening

**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Decision-maker:** Founder/CEO
**Time horizon:** 3 years
**Discount rate:** 18% (early-stage; reflects equity holders' opportunity cost)
**Capital constraint:** $180,000 year-0 outlay cap
**Strategic objectives:** Reach $2m ARR by end of 2027; reduce concentration risk (top customer = 38% of revenue); preserve fundraising optionality

---

## 1. Decision Context

The business is a 4-year-old B2B SaaS with $1.2m ARR, 78% gross margin, and a single product targeting financial controllers at mid-market firms. The founder has $180k of unspent capital from the last raise and is choosing between (A) building a sibling product for HR managers, (B) doubling down on the existing controllers segment with deeper integrations and an enterprise SKU, or (C) a hybrid that allocates capital across both. The decision is fundable from cash; no new raise required if a single bet is made.

## 9. Break-point Analysis

- **HR conversion rate:** If new-logo conversion falls below 65% of plan, Expand drops below Hybrid; below 45%, Expand turns negative. The plan currently assumes founder warm-intro yield of 25% — historical conversion in core market is 18%.
- **Discount rate:** At rates below 14%, the gap between Expand and Deepen narrows but Deepen still wins financially. At rates above 22%, Expand becomes marginal.
- **Concentration weight:** If concentration weight drops below 0.15 (from 0.30), Deepen overtakes Expand on the composite ranking.

## 10. Recommendation

**Withheld pending validation.** This is a case where the assumptions are too thin to recommend.

The analysis surfaces a clean trade-off: Deepen delivers the higher NPV but actively worsens the #1 stated strategic objective (concentration); Expand serves the strategy but rests on an unvalidated assumption (HR conversion rate via warm intros).

What would change the call:
- If the founder runs 20 HR-buyer discovery calls and yields ≥5 warm leads, Expand's assumption firms up and it becomes the recommendation.
- If the founder revisits the concentration objective and accepts that defensibility + scale matter more than diversification at this stage, Deepen becomes the recommendation.

This decision is NOT solving the fundraising optionality question. Investors will look at growth rate and net retention, both of which favour Deepen short-term. The Expand path's narrative ("we've shown we can do this twice") only pays off if the second product reaches meaningful scale within 18 months.

**Recommended next step:** 2-week customer-discovery sprint on the HR thesis before committing capital. If discovery is positive, Expand. If discovery is weak, Deepen and accept the concentration trade-off explicitly. Do NOT pick Hybrid — it is the worst of both options, capital-inefficient, and the analysis confirms it.

## 12. Biases to Watch

- **Sunk cost** — None material; this is a forward-looking allocation
- **Anchoring** — The CRO network's enthusiasm anchored the HR conversion assumption upward; we re-grounded with historical core-market conversion rates
- **Scope creep** — Hybrid option's scope grew during analysis until both threads were under-funded; we documented this and the analysis itself argues against Hybrid
- **Optimism / planning fallacy** — Expand's 6-month build assumes no scope discovery during the build; historical analogue (current product's MVP) took 9 months. Downside scenario applies +50% time
- **Status-quo bias** — Do-nothing was modelled with the probability-weighted top-customer churn risk to avoid treating $1.2m ARR as a safe floor

## When NOT to Use This Analysis

The diverging rankings show that this decision sits at the intersection of finance and strategy, and a single CBA cannot resolve it. Treat this report as input to a founder conversation about the strategy itself, not as a recommendation engine. The two-week discovery sprint is the right next move; locking in either Expand or Deepen now risks an irreversible commitment on an unvalidated assumption.
