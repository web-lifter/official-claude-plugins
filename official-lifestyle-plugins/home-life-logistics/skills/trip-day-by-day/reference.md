# Trip Day-by-Day — Reference Material

## Walking-Cap Heuristics (km/day)

| Party | Cap |
|-------|-----|
| Adults only, fit | 12 km |
| Adults only, mixed fitness | 8 km |
| Adults + older kids (8+) | 8 km |
| Adults + younger kids (4–7) | 5 km |
| Adults + toddler/pram | 4 km |
| Grandparents present | 4 km |
| Mixed-age group (3 generations) | 5 km — lowest mobility wins |

Add 30% if a lot is uphill or cobblestone (Italian hill towns, Lisbon, Kyoto).

## Hotel-Change Cadence

- **Single-city stays** (3+ nights per stop) — recommended for families and over-55s; cuts logistics overhead
- **Multi-city with overnight legs** — fine for adults under 50, fit and used to travel
- **Avoid:** moving hotels every night — exhausting; lost belongings risk

## AU-Specific Pre-Departure Resources

- **Smartraveller** (smartraveller.gov.au) — destination advice + register your trip
- **DFAT consular emergency** +61 2 6261 3305 — 24/7 from overseas
- **Travel insurance** — make sure it covers your activities (e.g. ski, scuba, motorbike)
- **PBS Safety Net + medication letter** — controlled meds need a GP letter for some destinations
- **TGA medication checker** — some AU OTC items are prescription elsewhere (e.g. codeine, pseudoephedrine)
- **Roaming** — Telstra Day Pass / Optus Travel Pack / Vodafone Roaming OR pocket WiFi OR local SIM

## Pacing Rules of Thumb

1. **Day 1 = arrival day = rest day.** Always.
2. **Every 4 days = a rest day.** Even if "nothing's planned" feels wasteful.
3. **One activity in the morning, one after lunch, one in the evening — max.** Cramming more = bickering by day 3.
4. **First and last hour of opening = best.** Crowds peak 10:30–14:00.
5. **Eat dinner earlier than you'd like.** 18:00–19:00 saves both energy and queue time.
6. **One "buffer" half-day per week.** Use it to revisit a favourite or do laundry.
