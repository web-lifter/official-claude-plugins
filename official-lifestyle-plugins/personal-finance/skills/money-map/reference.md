# Money Map — Reference Material

## AU Bank Automation Patterns

### Banks with strong sub-account / "bucket" features

| Bank | Feature | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Up** | Savers (unlimited sub-accounts), Maybuy goals, round-ups | Best UX; great for goal-based saving |
| **ING** | Multiple Savings Maximisers | Up to 9 accounts; bonus rate conditions matter |
| **Macquarie** | Multiple transaction + savings accounts | Strong app; sub-account flexibility |
| **86 400 / ubank** | Multiple bucket savers | Bonus-rate based |
| **CommBank** | NetBank Saver, "Goal Saver" | Limited sub-account UX; fine if already a customer |
| **NAB Reward Saver** | One bonus-rate account | Less granular |

### Standard pay-day rule patterns

1. **Bills first** — bills account funded before discretionary spending
2. **Savings buffer to a different bank** — friction-add to discourage transfers back
3. **Sinking funds bundle in one savings account, tracked in a sheet** — reduces account proliferation
4. **Investment + super top-up automated same day** — pay yourself first

### Vehicle
- Registration + CTP — usually annual

## Framework Quick-Reference

### 50/30/20
- 50% needs (rent, utilities, groceries, transport, insurance, minimum debt)
- 30% wants (dining, entertainment, hobbies, subscriptions)
- 20% savings + debt extra

### Zero-based
Every dollar is assigned a category. Income − allocations = $0. Best for tight budgets and behavioural change.

### Profit-first (sole-trader)
- Profit % first (e.g. 5–10%)
- Owner pay % second
- Tax % third
- Operating expenses last

### Modified for AU
- HECS-HELP is treated as a fixed (regulated) deduction — bake it into net income, not into discretionary or savings.
- Salary-sacrifice super sits in "investment / super top-up", not "savings" — different tax treatment.
- Centrelink payments (Family Tax Benefit, Carer Payment, etc.) — treat as fortnightly income, but flag review at means-test thresholds.
