## Analyst Notes — Things Worth Knowing

### Sequence-of-returns risk

The single largest risk to your retire-at-60 plan is not the long-term average return — it's the *order* in which returns arrive. A −10% year in your second year of drawdown can cost you 5+ years of sustainability. Building a 2–3 year cash buffer outside super and adopting a variable-spending rule are high-leverage mitigations.

### Tax-effectiveness of vehicle mix

You're heavily super-weighted (~80% super, ~20% ETF). At your income, salary sacrificing to the concessional cap saves you ~$5,200/yr in tax. You have meaningful headroom ($30k cap − $16.7k SG = $13.3k of unused concessional contribution capacity).

### Super-specific watch-outs

- **Concessional cap:** $30k/yr (current). You're at $16.7k via SG; $13.3k headroom.
- **Division 293:** kicks in at $250k income. Not currently applicable.
- **Transfer balance cap:** $1.9M (indexed). Optimistic scenario approaches it; base does not.
- **Co-contribution:** doesn't apply — income too high.

### Behavioural risks to plan for

A 30% drawdown is normal in long timeframes (2008, 2020). The temptation is to switch to "cash" at the bottom. Pre-commit by writing your investment-policy statement now while calm.
