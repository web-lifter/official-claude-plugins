# Future Me Projection — Reference Material

## AU Super Rules Summary (2026 FY — verify with ATO each year)

### Contribution Caps

- **Concessional (pre-tax):** $30,000/yr
  - Includes employer SG (currently 11.5%, rising to 12% on 01/07/2025)
  - Includes salary sacrifice
  - Includes personal deductible contributions
  - Taxed at 15% on entry (or 30% if Division 293 applies)
- **Non-concessional (post-tax):** $120,000/yr
  - Or "bring forward" $360,000 over 3 years (under-75 only)
  - Cap is conditional on Total Super Balance (TSB) at start of FY
- **Carry-forward concessional:** unused cap can roll forward 5 years if TSB < $500k at start of FY

### Division 293

- Additional 15% tax on concessional contributions when "Division 293 income" exceeds $250,000/yr

### Transfer Balance Cap

- $1,900,000 (indexed) — limit on how much can be transferred to a tax-free retirement-phase pension account
- Indexed in $100k increments to CPI

### Preservation Age

- Born after 01/07/1964 → preservation age 60

### Total Super Balance (TSB)

- Affects non-concessional cap eligibility
- Above $1.9M TSB at start of FY → no non-concessional contributions allowed

### Government Co-contribution

- For low-income earners ($43,445–$58,445 in 2024-25 FY; indexed)
- Government adds up to $500 per year of non-concessional contributions
- Phases out at higher income

### Downsizer Contribution

- Sale of primary home, age 55+
- Up to $300,000 each (couple = $600k)
- Doesn't count toward non-concessional cap

## Drawdown Frameworks

### 4% Rule (Trinity Study)

- Withdraw 4% of starting balance, indexed to inflation each year
- Historically sustains 30-year drawdown with high success rate (US data; AU markets slightly more volatile)

### Guyton-Klinger Guard-Rails (variable spending)

- Start at 4% withdrawal
- If portfolio drops 20% below initial, cut spending 10%
- If portfolio rises 20% above initial, increase spending 10%
- Historically supports higher initial withdrawals (4.5–5.5%) with lower failure rate

### Bond/Cash Ladder

- 2–3 years of expenses in cash/short bonds outside the volatile portfolio
- Replenish from portfolio in *good* years; spend from cash in *bad* years
- Mitigates sequence-of-returns risk

## Common Pitfalls When Projecting

1. **Single-point projections** — always show a range
2. **Ignoring real vs nominal** — banks quote nominal; users intuit real
3. **Optimistic return assumptions** — most retail super fund "long-term return" assumptions are net of fees
4. **Linear contribution assumptions** — most users increase contribution rate as income grows
5. **Ignoring sequence risk** — a 30-year average return obscures the brutal first 5 drawdown years
6. **Treating super as the whole picture** — PPOR equity, outside-super investments, expected inheritance, downsizing — all relevant
7. **Forgetting tax** — concessional contributions are tax-effective; non-concessional are not; outside-super gains are taxed; super pension is tax-free after 60
