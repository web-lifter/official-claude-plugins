## Strategy Comparison — N/A

For a HECS-HELP-only debt profile, the avalanche-vs-snowball comparison **doesn't apply** in the usual sense:

- HECS-HELP is **not interest-bearing** in the conventional sense. It's **indexed to CPI** (or wage-price index from 1 June 2025, whichever is lower).
- There is no minimum payment in the credit-card sense. Compulsory repayments come out of tax automatically once income exceeds a threshold (~$54k for the 2024-25 FY).
- **Voluntary repayments do not normally reduce the indexation amount** for the year the indexation is applied — they reduce the balance going forward.

**Recommendation: do nothing extra.** Pay only the compulsory amount via tax. Direct any "extra" capacity to higher-leverage moves.

## Why voluntary HECS repayment is usually suboptimal

| Alternative use of the same $1,000 | Likely benefit | Versus voluntary HECS |
|-----------------------------------|----------------|-----------------------|
| **Emergency buffer (up to 4-6 months expenses)** | Reduces catastrophic-debt risk | ✓ Higher priority |
| **Mortgage offset / first home deposit (FHSS)** | 6% interest saved (mortgage) or government tax discount (FHSS) | ✓ Higher priority |
| **Concessional super contribution** (if income > $45k) | Marginal tax rate − 15% saved (≈ 17.5% for $45k-$135k bracket) | ✓ Higher priority |
| **ETF / index investment** (10y horizon) | ~7% real return historically | ✓ Higher priority over 10+ years |
| **Voluntary HECS** | Saves ~3-4% indexation on remaining balance | ✗ Lower than all the above |

The **only situations** where voluntary HECS makes sense:
1. You're about to apply for a home loan and the lender treats HECS as a fixed monthly expense, reducing borrowing power (paying down lowers monthly compulsory repayment).
2. You're emigrating permanently and want to clear it before leaving Australia (still required to repay from overseas, but logistics).
3. You're a few thousand dollars from zero and want it gone for psychological cleanliness — fine if all higher-priority items are funded.

## Risk Plan

If Imogen's income drops below the repayment threshold (~$54k):

1. **Compulsory repayments pause automatically** — no action needed
2. **Indexation continues** — balance still grows with CPI
3. **No "default" event** — HECS-HELP cannot put you in collections; cannot affect credit score; cannot cause bankruptcy
4. **Indefinite suspension is OK** — there is no time limit on repayment

This is one of the most lenient debt structures in the world. Treating it like a credit card is a common mistake.
