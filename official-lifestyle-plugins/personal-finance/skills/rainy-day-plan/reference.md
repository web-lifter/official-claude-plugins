# Rainy Day Plan — Reference Material

## Insurance Gap Audit — Detailed Rule of Thumb

### Life insurance

- **Need:** Replace income for dependants + clear debts
- **Formula:** Annual gross income × 10 + total debts (incl. mortgage) − existing super/savings
- **Cheapest channel:** Within super (default + voluntary top-up)
- **Watch-outs:** Definition of "any" vs "own" occupation for TPD attachment; underwriting age cut-offs

### TPD (Total & Permanent Disability)

- **Need:** Lump sum if permanently unable to work
- **Formula:** Similar to life
- **Definition matters:** "Own occupation" is more useful but more expensive; "any occupation" pays only if you can't do *any* job

### Income protection

- **Need:** Replace 75% of income if you can't work temporarily
- **Key levers:**
  - Waiting period — 30 / 60 / 90 days (longer = cheaper)
  - Benefit period — 2 years / 5 years / to age 65 (longer = much pricier)
  - Indemnity vs agreed value — agreed is better but more expensive
- **Inside super:** ATO rules limit benefits to ~75% income; can run out at age 67
- **Outside super:** more flexibility, premiums tax-deductible

### Trauma / Critical illness

- **Need:** Lump sum on diagnosis of named condition (cancer, stroke, heart attack, etc.)
- **Typical cover:** $200k+
- **Useful for:** Covering uninsured medical expenses, time away from work beyond income protection waiting

### Private health

- **Hospital cover:** Avoids Medicare Levy Surcharge if income > thresholds (~$93k single / $186k family — indexed)
- **Extras:** Usually doesn't pay back the premium unless heavy users; consider opting for basic hospital + self-funding extras

### Home + contents

- **Rebuild value:** Use insurer's calculator + 10% buffer
- **Contents:** Inventory; underestimate is common
- **Flood / fire / cyclone:** Check region-specific endorsements

## Centrelink Quick Reference (verify with Services Australia each year)

### JobSeeker

- Eligibility: AU resident, looking for work, income/assets below thresholds
- 7-day waiting period (sometimes more for liquid assets)
- Payment varies by household, kids, etc.
- Income test; mutual obligation requirements

### Family Tax Benefit Part A + B

- Family with kids under 16 (FTB A) / single income or low-earner secondary (FTB B)
- Income-tested

### Carer Payment / Allowance

- Caring for someone with disability, illness, or aged

### Sickness Allowance

- (Now under JobSeeker — temporarily unable to work due to illness)

### Concessions

- Energy supplement, pharmaceutical (PBS Safety Net), dental (state-based)
- Many automatic with Pensioner Concession Card / Health Care Card

## Lender Hardship Pathways

- **Mortgage:** Contact lender's hardship team immediately. Options include payment deferral, interest-only switch, extended term, refinance to a longer term. AFCA (Australian Financial Complaints Authority) free escalation if dispute.
- **Personal loan / credit card:** Same — hardship team. Most have formal hardship programs.
- **ATO:** Payment plans available; serious hardship release in rare cases. Call ATO 13 11 42 or use ATO Online services.
- **Utilities (energy, telco):** Hardship programs at every major retailer (mandatory under AU regulations); contact retailer + check energymadeeasy.gov.au for switching.

## Common Underinsurance Patterns

1. **Life — sole earner with mortgage > 5× income** — typically grossly underinsured by default super cover
2. **No income protection** — single biggest gap in most AU households with dependants
3. **TPD "any occupation" only** — pays nothing if you can do *some* job, even if can't do your career
4. **Home rebuild estimate from purchase years ago** — construction costs increased significantly; underinsurance common
5. **Contents listed at "feels right" rather than itemised** — usually 30–50% low
6. **No private health hospital and earning > MLS threshold** — paying MLS for no cover
