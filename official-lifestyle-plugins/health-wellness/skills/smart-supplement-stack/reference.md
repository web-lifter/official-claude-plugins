# Smart Supplement Stack — Reference Material

## Evidence Grading (Examine.com style)

| Grade | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **A** | Strong evidence — multiple RCTs + meta-analysis; effect is robust |
| **B** | Moderate — several RCTs, mostly positive; some heterogeneity |
| **C** | Weak / mixed — small studies, contradictory results, or only observational |
| **D** | Anecdotal / no evidence — do not recommend |

This plugin does not recommend D-grade items.

## Supplement Quick Reference

### A-grade (strong evidence)

| Supplement | Dose | Best for | Notes |
|-----------|------|----------|-------|
| Creatine monohydrate | 3–5g/day | Strength, power, cognition | No loading needed; cheapest generic; safe long-term |
| Caffeine | 1–3 mg/kg | Endurance, focus | Pre-training only; not after 12pm; tolerance builds |
| Vitamin D3 | 1000–4000 IU/day | Bone, immune, mood (if deficient) | Test serum; AU adults frequently deficient in winter |
| Omega-3 (EPA + DHA combined) | 1–2g/day | Cardiovascular, inflammation, mood | Look for combined ≥ 1g; refrigerate after opening |
| Protein powder (whey / soy / pea) | Top-up to 1.6–2.2 g/kg | Muscle protein synthesis | Food first; powder fills convenience gaps |
| Nicotine (small, controlled) | — | Cognition (acute) | **Highly addictive; recommend caution; not endorsed for non-users** |

### B-grade (moderate evidence)

| Supplement | Dose | Best for | Notes |
|-----------|------|----------|-------|
| Magnesium glycinate / citrate | 200–400mg | Sleep, cramps, anxiety | Glycinate gentler; oxide cheap but laxative |
| Beta-alanine | 3–5g/day | Endurance over 60s–4min | Tingling normal; builds over weeks |
| Ashwagandha (KSM-66) | 600mg/day | Stress, sleep, modest testosterone | Cycle 8 weeks on / 2 off |
| Curcumin (with piperine) | 500–1000mg | Joint discomfort | Bioavailability needs piperine or phospholipid form |
| L-theanine | 100–200mg | Calm focus (with caffeine) | Smooths caffeine; works acutely |
| Iron (if tested low) | 18mg ferrous bisglycinate | Iron-deficient | **Only if blood test confirms deficiency**; not prophylactic |

### C-grade (weak / mixed)

| Supplement | Dose | Best for | Notes |
|-----------|------|----------|-------|
| Multivitamin | 1/day | Insurance | OK if diet is variable; not a substitute for food |
| Zinc | 15–30mg | Immune | Pulse during illness; not daily long-term |
| BCAAs | 5–10g | "Recovery" | Redundant if total protein adequate |
| Glutamine | 5g | "Recovery" | Insufficient evidence for healthy lifters |
| Tribulus | — | "Testosterone" | No evidence for healthy men |
| Apple cider vinegar | — | Various | Mostly hype |

### D-grade (not recommended)

- Detox teas, juice cleanses, "fat burners", proprietary "anti-aging" stacks, hemp/CBD without specific medical indication, unstudied "nootropics".

## Common Interactions Matrix

| Item | Conflicts with | Action |
|------|---------------|--------|
| Calcium | Iron, magnesium, zinc | Separate by ≥ 2 hours |
| Iron | Coffee, tea, calcium, eggs | Take 1h before/2h after; consider vitamin C boost |
| Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) | Fasted state | Take with a fat-containing meal |
| Stimulants (caffeine + others) | Each other | Don't stack pre-workout + coffee + ECA |
| Sedatives + magnesium / valerian / ashwagandha | Sleep meds | Risk of compounding sedation — check with pharmacist |
| Fish oil + blood thinners | Warfarin, aspirin | Check with GP for high doses |
| SSRIs + St John's Wort + 5-HTP | Serotonin syndrome risk | **Avoid combining; GP referral required** |

## Pregnancy / Breastfeeding Restrictions

**Always refer to OBGYN / GP. Do not produce a new-stack recommendation for these users.**

Commonly contraindicated or requiring clinician sign-off:

- Vitamin A retinol > 700mcg/day
- Herbal supplements (most untested in pregnancy)
- High-dose fish oil
- Ashwagandha, kava, St John's Wort
- Saw palmetto
- Stimulants (caffeine limit ≤ 200mg/day)

Commonly **recommended** by clinicians:

- Folate / folic acid (pre-conception + first trimester) — typically 400–500mcg
- Iodine — typically 150mcg in pregnancy multivitamin
- Iron — only if tested low
- Vitamin D — if deficient

This skill **does not** prescribe pregnancy supplementation. Refer.

## AU TGA Notes

- Therapeutic claims (treats, cures, prevents) are regulated. This plugin uses neutral language ("supports", "may help").
- Look for the "AUST L" or "AUST R" listing number on AU-supplied bottles. AUST R indicates higher regulatory scrutiny.
- Be wary of imported supplements via marketplaces — purity and dosing can vary widely. Prefer pharmacy-stocked brands.
- Schedule 4 items (e.g. melatonin) require a prescription in Australia.
