## REFERRAL PATH — pregnancy + prescription meds

Priya is pregnant (first trimester) and on two prescription medications (sertraline 50mg, levothyroxine 75mcg). This skill **does not recommend new supplements** for users with this profile. Output is limited to (a) a current-stack audit and (b) a clinician-referral list.

### Why this is a refer-out

- Pregnancy alters drug metabolism and supplement safety profiles
- Multiple supplements are contraindicated or have insufficient pregnancy data
- Sertraline + St John's Wort = serotonin syndrome risk (catastrophic)
- Levothyroxine + calcium + iron + soy isoflavones = absorption interference
- Hyperthyroidism / autoimmune thyroid status changes through pregnancy — supplementation strategy depends on trimester-by-trimester thyroid panels

## Items requiring an OBGYN conversation this week

1. **Iodine intake** — confirm prenatal multi has 150 mcg; AU population is iodine-replete-borderline in pregnancy. Insufficient iodine in trimester 1 affects foetal neurodevelopment.
2. **DHA intake** — fish oil dose may need adjustment based on diet; OBGYN may recommend a dedicated DHA-only supplement.
3. **Iron status** — OBGYN typically orders a ferritin + Hb at the booking visit; supplementation decision should follow this.
4. **Thyroid panel** — TSH free T4 should be re-tested early in trimester 1 and again at 12–16 weeks; levothyroxine dose usually needs an upward adjustment in pregnancy.
5. **Calcium timing** — must be ≥ 4 hours from levothyroxine. Take Cal supplement (if needed) at lunch or dinner; take levothyroxine on waking on an empty stomach.
