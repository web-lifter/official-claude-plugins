## STOP — clinical referral required before any protocol

Phase 1 intake surfaced multiple red-flag indicators consistent with possible obstructive sleep apnoea (OSA). This skill **does not produce a sleep protocol for users with these indicators** — they need a sleep physician's diagnosis first.

### Red flags reported

| Indicator | Marco's response |
|-----------|------------------|
| Loud snoring (audible from another room) | Partner reports yes, multiple times/night |
| Witnessed pauses in breathing during sleep | Partner reports yes — "wakes himself up gasping" |
| Daytime sleepiness despite ≥ 7h in bed | Yes — falls asleep watching TV most evenings |

| BMI ≥ 30 | Yes (BMI ~32) |
| Hypertension or pre-hypertension | Yes — on treatment for 2 years |

This is a STOP-BANG screening profile with high probability of moderate-to-severe OSA. Untreated OSA is a cardiovascular and cognitive risk. The right next step is medical assessment, not a sleep-hygiene protocol.

## Recommended Referral Pathway

### Step 1 — GP visit this week

Book a GP appointment. Tell them:

> "My partner reports loud snoring and witnessed apnoea episodes. I have morning headaches and daytime sleepiness despite 7+ hours in bed. I'd like to be referred for a sleep study."

The GP will likely:
- Take the same STOP-BANG screening + Epworth Sleepiness Scale
- Refer to a sleep physician or directly to a home-based sleep study (Level 2 polysomnography)
- Order baseline bloods (TSH, fasting glucose, lipids — comorbidity check)

### Step 2 — Sleep study

Two paths in Australia:

| Path | Time | Cost (rough, AUD) |
|------|------|-------------------|
| **GP-referred bulk-billed home study** | 1–3 weeks | $0 with bulk-billing referral |
| **Specialist-referred in-lab polysomnography** | 4–8 weeks | $150–500 out-of-pocket with Medicare rebate |

### Step 3 — Diagnosis + treatment plan

Possible diagnoses + typical first-line treatments:

| Diagnosis | First-line | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Mild OSA | Positional therapy, weight loss, mandibular advancement device | Hygiene matters; this skill can help **after** GP review |
| Moderate-severe OSA | CPAP therapy | Significant cardiovascular protection |
| Central sleep apnoea (rarer) | Specialist-led | — |

### Step 4 — After diagnosis: revisit this skill

Once Marco has a diagnosis and a treatment plan in place, this skill **can usefully complement** the medical treatment by addressing the modifiable hygiene factors (sleep timing consistency, alcohol cutoff, bedroom environment, caffeine timing). Run the full Phase 2–4 protocol **after** the sleep physician approves.

## Until the GP visit

| Action | When | Why |
|--------|------|-----|
| Avoid alcohol within 4h of bed | Tonight onward | Alcohol worsens OSA |
| Sleep on side, not back | Tonight onward | Reduces airway collapse in positional OSA |
| Avoid sedatives + sleeping pills | Indefinitely | They worsen OSA severity |
| Continue prescribed blood-pressure medication | As prescribed | Don't change without GP review |
| Note daily energy (1–5) + AM headache (Y/N) | Daily until GP visit | Useful data for the GP |
| Do NOT begin a fitness program, fasting protocol, or sleep restriction therapy | Wait | These could destabilise an OSA presentation |
