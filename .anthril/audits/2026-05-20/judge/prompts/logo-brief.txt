## 5. Mark Type

**Recommended mark type:** Combination mark (Wordmark + simple botanical pictorial)

**Why this type:**
Verdant is a new brand with no recognition, so a pure pictorial mark would be incomprehensible. A pure wordmark would not register on a packed supermarket shelf at 3 metres. A combination mark — wordmark + a small, restrained botanical pictorial — gives the brand both legibility (at distance, the pictorial registers) and clarity (close up, the name reads). The pictorial element should be subtle enough that it can stand alone as the product-bottle stamp at 12mm.

**Alternative considered:** Pictorial alone — rejected because new brand recognition is too weak.

**Visual description:** A single, drawn-not-rendered leaf paired with a serif wordmark "verdant" set in lowercase. The leaf is one continuous line, no fill, in the brand primary green. The leaf sits to the left of the wordmark in horizontal lockup, and above the wordmark in stacked lockup. Leaf is the product stamp at small sizes.

**Style references:**
